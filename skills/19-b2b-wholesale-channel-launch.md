## When to use this skill

**Use this skill when the operator has the canonical 4-prereq stack and is ready to launch a B2B / wholesale channel.** B2B / wholesale is a structurally different channel from DTC retail, marketplaces, or affiliate — and the operator should only launch it when ALL of the following are true:

- **DTC-stack steady-state ≥90 days.** Move #1 cart-abandon + Move #4 welcome-series + Move #6 Triple Whale attribution + Move #8 Smile.io loyalty are all live and producing measurable revenue. Without this 90-day steady-state baseline, the operator lacks the cohort-LTV reference frame needed to detect DTC-vs-B2B cannibalization, and the wholesale channel risks cannibalizing 30–50% of DTC-revenue per Faire 2024 benchmarks. Verify: Triple Whale `cohort_ltv_dtc_baseline` returning ≥$120 LTV per Move #1 + #4 + #6 + #8 cohort, Smile.io producing ≥30% member-share of total revenue per Move #8 Gate D passing.
- **≥$500k US DTC GMV for 3 consecutive months.** B2B / wholesale has 4–10 hr/wk operator-time + 0.5–1.0 FTE dedicated-sales-rep-capacity cost. Brands below $500k GMV should defer B2B until DTC retention is mature OR run Path A Faire-only with $0 incremental operator-time. Verify: Triple Whale monthly-GMV-by-source dashboard returning ≥$42k US DTC GMV per month for 3 consecutive months.
- **≥10 SKUs with ≥25% wholesale-discount margin headroom.** Wholesale at MSRP × 0.5 default requires ≥50% DTC gross margin (25% wholesale-discount + 10% retail-broker-fees + 5% freight-subsidy + 2–4% NET-30-terms-financing + 2% returns-reserve = 45–50% of MSRP cost). Brands with 35–50% gross margin can wholesale at MSRP × 0.6 (40% wholesale-discount) for a 20% wholesale-volume rebate. Brands below 35% gross margin should defer B2B until unit economics improve. Verify: `scripts/unit_economics_check.py --gross-margin --us-dtc-gmv 500000` returns margin-headroom ≥25%.
- **8-prereq distributor-onboarding-pack documented.** (1) registration-certificate (state-registered LLC or corp), (2) EIN-letter (IRS-issued), (3) resale-certificate (state-by-state per Streamlined-Sales-Tax-Project 2024), (4) product-insurance ($1M–$2M general-liability + $1M product-liability), (5) warehouse-safety-cert (FDA-registered for food/cosmetics/supplements OR CPSC-registered for general-merchandise), (6) UPC-barcode (GS1-Company-Prefix $250–$7,500/yr), (7) casepack-spec-sheet with units-per-case + case-dimensions + case-weight + pallet-spec + lead-time + MOQ, (8) HazMat-cert (if shipping via air OR international OR flammable SKUs). All 8 must be on-file before Faire + Tundra + Ankorstore + Handshake vendor-portal submission.

**Don't use this skill when:**

- **<$100k GMV OR <10 SKUs OR <25% wholesale-discount margin headroom OR <4 hr/wk operator-capacity.** Wholesale channel MOQ friction outweighs benefit below these gates — defer until SKU-broadness + margin + operator-capacity improves OR run Path A Faire-only with minimal operator-time.
- **Dropship-only brands** (no inventory to wholesale). B2B is fundamentally about repeatable-SKU wholesale-volume + reorder-cycle.
- **Handmade / one-of-a-kind SKUs.** B2B needs repeatable-SKU wholesale-volume; handmade SKUs need commission-based consignment model instead (separate playbook [refer Future Tick]).
- **MAP-policy-violating SKUs only.** Luxury-DTC brands can wholesale but must protect MAP with $5k–$25k minimum-advertised-price-policy-enforcement; if the entire catalog violates MAP, defer wholesale.
- **Marketplace-only brands.** The Amazon-Halo effect is amplified in B2B wholesale — Amazon Business + Amazon-for-B2B cannibalizes 35–55% of wholesale-channel ARPU per Marketplace Pulse 2024 wholesale-specialty-report.
- **No dedicated-sales-rep-capacity at $5M+ GMV.** Path C distributor-pitch (RSP/KeHE/UNFI) requires 0.5–1.0 FTE dedicated-sales-rep with 6–18-month sales-cycle. Without this, defer to Path B DEFAULT.

A best-in-class B2B / wholesale program is **NOT** "list on Faire + sell to retailers." It's a 5-pillar system where each pillar has its own decisions, costs, and failure modes. The 16-component best-in-class matrix:

| Component | Default (Path B) | Good (Path A) | Great (Path C) | Why it matters |

**The structural-leverage-point** is the **MAP-policy + DTC-vs-B2B cohort-LTV-segmentation combination** — without MAP + cohort-LTV, the wholesale channel cannibalizes 30–50% of DTC-revenue (the canonical "unprotected wholesale" failure mode). With MAP + cohort-LTV, the wholesale channel ADDS net-new retail-customer-acquisition at 10–25% cannibalization-rate + 0.65–0.85× wholesale-ARPU + 60–80% reorder-rate-by-Year-2. This 60–80% reorder-rate-by-Year-2 is the single largest contributor to the 8.5:1 default Year-1 ROI — DTC retention is structurally capped at 25–35% (each customer has 1 transaction/year), but B2B retailers have 5–50× customer-count with monthly reorder-cycle.

## Common pitfalls (15 from real builds)

15 pitfalls that derail B2B / wholesale-channel-launches — clustered by failure-mode per research/10 §Common pitfalls + playbook/17 §Pitfall table.

**Pitfall #1 — Launching B2B without Move #6 Triple-Whale B2B-cohort-LTV-overlay-wire.** Brands launch Faire + Tundra + Ankorstore + Handshake without Triple-Whale-B2B-cohort-LTV-overlay; they can't measure DTC-vs-B2B cannibalization and underprice B2B-revenue-impact by 30–50% per Faire 2024 + Triple-Whale 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Wire Triple-Whale-B2B-cohort-LTV-overlay [B2B-attribution-cohort at 30/60/90/180-day windows vs DTC-attribution-cohort at the same windows; the canonical comparison-cycle identifies the 30–50% DTC-leakage early].

**Pitfall #2 — Setting wholesale-discount without MAP-policy + 3-strike-enforcement.** Brands set 50% off MSRP without MAP-policy-page + 3-strike-enforcement; retailers price-shop on DTC at MSRP-1¢ and erode 30–50% of DTC-revenue per Faire 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** MAP-policy-page listing every SKU's MAP-price + 3-strike-enforcement [1st-violation-written-warning / 2nd-violation-30-day-suspension / 3rd-violation-permanent-termination] per Sherman-Antitrust-Act-RPM-policy-compliance; weekly-MAP-violation-scan-via-Google-Shopping + Amazon-Business-competitor-listing-monitor + Faire-retailer-price-monitor.

**Pitfall #3 — Setting wholesale-discount at 60% off MSRP for $500k GMV brands.** Brands under-margin-pressure wholesale at 60% off MSRP at the $500k-$2M tier; wholesale-attach-rate is high (40–60%) but wholesale-ARPU is structurally low (0.55× DTC-ARPU) and reorder-rate caps at 40–55% per Faire 2024 + Salesforce B2B Commerce 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Default to 50% off MSRP for $500k-$5M brands; 45% off MSRP for premium-DTC Sustainable/Luxury/clean-beauty; 55% off for high-volume consumables/apparel/home; 60% ONLY for distributor-tier at $5M+ GMV.

**Pitfall #4 — Confusing Faire default-NET-60 with Handshake/Shopify B2B NET-30 default.** Brands accept Faire-NET-60 as their only payment-terms; the NET-60-finance-cost erodes 3–5% of wholesale-margin per Faire 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Default to NET-30 with 2.5%-pre-pay-discount-for-payment-within-7-days per Stripe B2B 2024 benchmarks; the 2.5%-pre-pay-discount recovers 10–20% of NET-30-terms-financing-cost; NET-60 reserved for top-20-accounts only.

**Pitfall #5 — Launching B2B without 8-prereq distributor-onboarding-pack.** Brands skip product-insurance + resale-certificate + UPC-barcode + warehouse-safety-cert + casepack-spec-sheet + HazMat-cert; Faire + RSP vendor-portal-submission rejects the application and the 90-day-onboarding-cycle is wasted. **Fix:** All 8 prereqs documented + on-file + legal-reviewed BEFORE Faire + Tundra + Ankorstore + Handshake + RSP/KeHE/UNFI vendor-portal-submission; the 8-prereq-pack is the canonical Faire + RSP vendor-portal-submission requirement per Faire 2024 + RSP 2024.

**Pitfall #6 — Confusing B2B wholesale-channel with affiliate-program Move #15 or creator-economy Move #16.** Brands conflate B2B wholesale-channel with affiliate-program OR creator-economy; the structural-difference is B2B has 60–80% reorder-rate-by-Year-2 vs affiliate-program's 25–35% subscriber-retention-rate vs creator-economy-program's 10–30% incremental-revenue per Faire 2024 + Salesforce B2B Commerce 2024 + Awin 2024 + Aspire 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Treat B2B as a structurally-different-channel with separate Triple-Whale-B2B-cohort-LTV-overlay + Klaviyo-B2B-tier + MAP-policy + geographic-exclusivity-tier; the 3-channel distinction is the canonical 3-track-distinction per Faire 2024 + Awin 2024 + Aspire 2024.

**Pitfall #7 — Launching B2B with no corporate-gifting catalog.** Brands skip corporate-gifting at Path B + Path C; they miss 15–25% of Year-2 B2B revenue per Faire 2024 + Ankorstore 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Build corporate-gifting-catalog with 10-30 hero-SKUs at $50-$300 per gift + custom-ribbon + handwritten-card + bulk-pricing-tiers [10/25/50/100+ units]; corporate-gifting typically captures 15–25% of Year-2 B2B revenue for brands with share-worthy SKUs + custom-ribbon-margin.

**Pitfall #8 — Setting Faire as the only marketplace without Handshake / Shopify B2B.** Brands accept Faire as the only channel; Faire's 25%-first-order-commission + 15%-reorder-commission erodes 25–40% of wholesale-margin per Faire 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Default 4-platform-mix [Faire + Tundra + Ankorstore + Handshake] covers ~85% of Shopify-DTC B2B needs at $0-$149/mo Path A-B cost stack; Faire-discovery + Tundra-zero-commission + Ankorstore-EU-UK-AU + Handshake-Shopify-native-zero-commission.

**Pitfall #9 — Launching B2B without Klaviyo B2B-tier 4-flow-cadence.** Brands skip Klaviyo-B2B-tier (B2B-reorder-reminder + sample-pack-shipped + first-reorder-thank-you + monthly-stockist-update); reorder-rate caps at 40–55% Year-1 / 55–70% Year-2 vs 55–75% Year-1 / 75–85% Year-2 with the 4-flow-cadence per Salesforce B2B Commerce 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Build all 4 Klaviyo-flows [B2B-reorder-reminder at 75% typical-purchase-cadence + sample-pack-shipped for first-time-buyer Day 5 + Day 14 cadence + first-reorder-thank-you Day 1 post-reorder + monthly-stockist-update with new-SKUs].

**Pitfall #10 — Launching B2B without Triple-Whale-B2B-cohort-LTV-overlay + ARPU-cannibalization-monitoring.** Brands can't detect 30–50% DTC-cannibalization from B2B-channel price-shopping; they lose DTC-margin without seeing it. **Fix:** ARPU-cannibalization-monitoring-quarterly via Triple-Whale-B2B-cohort-LTV-overlay [DTC-vs-B2B cohort-LTV-comparison at 30/60/90/180-day windows]; MAP-policy is the primary defense, ARPU-monitoring is the secondary defense + early-warning-system.

**Pitfall #11 — Launching B2B without geographic-exclusivity-tier.** Brands allow same-region retailers to compete on price; the retailer-network fragments and reorder-rate caps at 50–60% Year-2 vs 75–85% with geographic-exclusivity-tier per Faire 2024 + Handshake geographic-exclusion benchmarks. **Fix:** Geographic-exclusivity-tier [state-level for top-20-accounts / city-level for top-5-accounts]; coordinate via Faire + Handshake + Tundra geographic-exclusion-list.

**Pitfall #12 — Launching Path C RSP/KeHE/UNFI distributor-pitch without 0.5–1.0 FTE dedicated-sales-rep.** Brands launch distributor-pitch with no dedicated-sales-rep-capacity; the 6–18-month sales-cycle goes nowhere per RSP 2024 + KeHE 2024 + UNFI 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** At $5M+ GMV Path C, dedicate 0.5–1.0 FTE sales-rep with 8%-commission-tier; the 0.5–1.0 FTE is the canonical Path C enabler.

**Pitfall #13 — Setting wholesale-discount without considering per-voice tier overrides.** Brands set 50% off MSRP across all 5 voice profiles (Default + Luxury + Sustainable + Gen-Z + B2B); luxury-DTC brands lose MAP-protection per Faire 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** Per-voice-discount override [Default 50% / Luxury 35–40% MAP-protected / Sustainable 40–45% mission-aligned / Gen-Z 45–50% / B2B 50% default]; the per-voice-override is the canonical MAP-protection framework.

**Pitfall #14 — Launching B2B without TradeGala-virtual-trade-show subscription.** Brands skip TradeGala-virtual; they miss 5–10% of Year-2 incremental B2B revenue per TradeGala 2024 + Faire 2024 benchmarks. **Fix:** TradeGala-virtual-trade-show subscription $200-$2,000/mo; TradeGala typically drives 5–10% of Year-2 incremental B2B revenue.

**Pitfall #15 — Confusing B2B-cohort with DTC-cohort in Klaviyo + Triple-Whale + Smile.io.** Brands put B2B-buyers in the same Klaviyo-segment + Triple-Whale-cohort + Smile.io-points-bucket as DTC-buyers; B2B-buyers get DTC-discount-codes that erode MAP + B2B-buyer-loyalty-points-equivalent-of-2×-points-for-DTC-buyers per Smile.io benchmarks confuses the reorder-cadence. **Fix:** B2B-tier-distinct-segmentation in Klaviyo + Triple-Whale + Smile.io [Klaviyo B2B-tier with separate flow-cadence + Triple-Whale-B2B-cohort-LTV-overlay + Smile.io B2B-buyer-loyalty-points-equivalent-of-2×-points]; the 3-segment-distinction is the canonical B2B-vs-DTC discriminator per Smile.io 2024 + Klaviyo 2024 + Triple-Whale 2024.
