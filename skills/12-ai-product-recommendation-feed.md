# AI product recommendation feed (Nosto + Rebuy + Dynamic Yield, Move #20.2)

> The single highest-leverage Move #20 sub-track for $1M+ DTC brands that have already shipped Move #2 post-purchase-upsell. An AI-personalized recommendation feed lifts AOV 5–15%, lifts repeat-purchase-rate 10–30%, lifts 12-month subscriber LTV 15–25%, and unlocks the same Triple-Whale cohort-LTV-overlay math as Move #14 lifecycle-marketing — but at the PDP / cart / post-purchase surface where decisions are made, not in an email 6 hours later. Ship Nosto or Rebuy with a 3-phase rollout at 6:1–25:1 Year-1 ROI. Move #20.2 — the canonical Pillar-5 deep-dive of Move #20 generative-AI-engine that compounds Move #2 + Move #11 + Move #14 + Move #6 attribution-substrate.

## When to use this skill

You have:

- A Shopify (or Ikas / BigCommerce / WooCommerce) store with ≥1,000 SKUs and ≥6 months of order history
- Move #2 post-purchase-upsell shipped (ReConvert / AfterSell / Bold Upsell) — Move #20.2 compounds it 2–3×
- Move #6 Triple Whale (or Polar) attribution live — required for AI-rec cohort-LTV-overlay math
- Move #5 Klaviyo + Move #7 Postscript wired — required for "you might also like" flow triggers
- Move #10 AI ad creative iteration shipped ≥3 months — ≥50 creatives/wk baseline (the move #20 prereq)
- ≤$50 average-AOV brands should consider cheaper Move #2 + #14 only; ≥$50 AOV is the move #20.2 sweet spot

You're NOT a fit if:

- You're <$500k GMV — defer until Move #1 + #4 + #5 + #6 + #7 + #10 ship
- You have <1,000 SKUs — rule-based "frequently bought together" via ReConvert is enough
- You're a single-product brand — recommendation engine has nothing to recommend
- You have no Triple Whale attribution — you can't measure rec-cohort LTV without it

## What "best in class" looks like

A 2026-vintage DTC PDP / cart / post-purchase surface where every customer sees a personalized "you might also like" / "complete the look" / "your vibe matched" / "customers who bought X also bought Y" feed that **adapts in real-time** based on (a) the customer's last 5 viewed products, (b) the customer's last 3 purchases, (c) the customer's loyalty tier, (d) the customer's SMS-cohort-LTV-band from Triple Whale. Best-in-class brands don't ship a single "Recommended for you" widget — they ship **7 contextual surfaces** each powered by the same AI engine:

| Surface | Where it shows | Best-in-class behavior | Median baseline |
|---|---|---|---|
| PDP "complete the look" | Below the buy box | "Frequently bought together" + personal rec; lifts AOV 8–12% | Static "you might also like" carousels |
| PDP "customers also viewed" | Below reviews | Co-browse correlation: "People who viewed X also viewed Y"; +5–8% CVR | Generic "trending" widget |
| Cart drawer "add a little more" | Inside cart drawer | 1 personalized add-on based on cart contents; +6–10% AOV | Static "bundle and save" widget |
| Cart upsell post-ATC | After "added to cart" toast | "Wait — there's a perfect match for that"; +10–15% AOV | Generic cross-sell page |
| Post-purchase cross-sell | ReConvert / AfterSell page | "Customers who bought [X] also bought [Y]"; +12–20% AOV lift | Static "complete the set" widget |
| Email "recommended for you" | Klaviyo flow block at day 7 / day 21 / day 60 | Personalized per-customer rec; +20–40% email CTR vs static blocks | "Top sellers" block |
| SMS "your vibe matched" | Postscript cart-abandon follow-up | One personalized product suggestion per SMS; +15–25% SMS CVR | Generic "shop new arrivals" link |

Per Nosto 2024 + Rebuy 2024 + Dynamic Yield 2024 + Sailthru 2024 benchmarks: brands that ship the 7-surface AI-rec stack lift **AOV 8–15%, conversion rate 4–8%, and 12-month repeat-purchase rate 12–25%** vs static-recommendation baselines. The compounding math: a $5M GMV brand at $60 AOV with 14% repeat-purchase rate capturing the median AOV lift (10%) + median repeat-purchase lift (15%) earns **$500k–$1.2M Year-1 incremental revenue** at **6:1–25:1 Year-1 ROI** with $1k–$5k/mo total platform cost.

## AI product recommendation benchmarks (2024–25)

The 3-path decision matrix matches the canonical Move #20 pattern: pick based on GMV tier + tech-stack fit + AI-rec-capability needed.

### Path A — Fresh-launch / pre-$1M GMV ($0–$200/mo cost stack)

- **Default platform:** Rebuy Starter $0–$249/mo OR Nosto Starter $0–$159/mo
- **AI capability:** Rule-based "frequently bought together" + "recently viewed" rec engine
- **Year-1 cost:** $0–$2,400 platform + $500–$2k operator time = **$500–$4,400 total Year-1 cost**
- **Year-1 incremental revenue:** $50k–$200k (AOV lift 5–10% + repeat-purchase-rate lift 5–15%)
- **Year-1 ROI band:** 12:1–80:1 (median ~25:1 — the sweet spot for small brands)

### Path B — DEFAULT $1M–$25M GMV DTC ($1k–$5k/mo cost stack)

- **Default platform:** Rebuy Pro $499/mo OR Nosto Growth $499/mo OR Dynamic Yield Standard $1,000+/mo
- **AI capability:** ML-personalized "you might also like" + loyalty-tier-segment-overlay + SMS-cohort-LTV-band-routing + 7-surface widget
- **Year-1 cost:** $6k–$60k platform + $5k–$15k operator time = **$11k–$75k total Year-1 cost**
- **Year-1 incremental revenue:** $200k–$1.5M (AOV lift 8–15% + repeat-purchase-rate lift 10–25% + cohort-LTV-multiplier 1.15–1.25× on rec-engaged cohort)
- **Year-1 ROI band:** 6:1–25:1 (median ~12:1) with 3-month payback (the canonical Path B leverage band)

### Path C — $25M+ enterprise / multi-brand / international ($3k–$15k/mo cost stack)

- **Default platform:** Dynamic Yield Enterprise $5k+/mo OR Algolia Recommend Enterprise $3k+/mo OR Nosto Enterprise $2k+/mo (custom pricing)
- **AI capability:** Full ML-rec engine + cohort-LTV-multiplier math + custom-trained-LLM-on-brand-voice-data-product-description-iteration (compounds Move #20.1 AI-customer-service-automation brand-voice) + international-multi-locale-rec (5+ Shopify Markets locales)
- **Year-1 cost:** $36k–$180k platform + $25k–$50k operator time = **$61k–$230k total Year-1 cost**
- **Year-1 incremental revenue:** $1M–$5M (AOV lift 10–15% + repeat-purchase-rate lift 15–25% + international-rec-cohort-LTV-multiplier 1.25–1.50× + 12–24-month LTV-compounding)
- **Year-1 ROI band:** 5:1–15:1 (median ~10:1, muted by 6–12-month build cycle)

**Default: Path B** for $1M–$25M DTC brands at ≥$50 AOV with ≥1,000 SKUs and Triple Whale wired. Path A for fresh-launch. Path C only at $25M+ GMV with dedicated martech team.

## The build (8 steps over 8–14 weeks)

### Step 1 — Install + baseline (Week 1, ~2 hr)

1. Install Rebuy (or Nosto / Dynamic Yield) on Shopify from the app store; authenticate with admin role
2. Wire the platform-side pixel + customer-ID-cookie + viewed-product-event + ATC-event + checkout-event (Rebuy: native; Nosto: 5-min install; Dynamic Yield: 30–60 min)
3. Connect Klaviyo (or your ESP) for email-recommendation flows
4. Connect Postscript (or your SMS) for SMS-recommendation flows
5. Triple Whale: enable the **rec-engaged cohort** tag (every customer who clicks or purchases a rec widget in the next 30 days gets tagged `rec_engaged: true`)

**Verification snapshot:** rec-engine logs show ≥95% of PDP pageviews received a recommendation within 200ms of page render.

### Step 2 — Build the 7-surface widget baseline (Weeks 2–3, ~6 hr)

1. **Surface 1 — PDP "complete the look"** (Rebuy: PDP Smart Widget; Nosto: Recommendation PDP block): personal rec + co-bought correlation
2. **Surface 2 — PDP "customers also viewed"** (Rebuy: PDP Smart Widget variant; Nosto: "Related Products" block): co-browse correlation
3. **Surface 3 — Cart drawer "add a little more"** (Rebuy native; Nosto: Cart Drawer block): 1 personalized add-on based on cart contents
4. **Surface 4 — Cart upsell post-ATC** (Rebuy: ATC Smart Widget; Nosto: "Inspired by your cart"): "Wait — there's a perfect match for that"
5. **Surface 5 — Post-purchase cross-sell** (Rebuy + ReConvert integration; Nosto: Thank You block): "Customers who bought X also bought Y"
6. **Surface 6 — Email "recommended for you"** (Klaviyo flow block at day 7 / day 21 / day 60): per-customer personalized feed
7. **Surface 7 — SMS "your vibe matched"** (Postscript cart-abandon follow-up, day 2): one personalized product per SMS

**Verification snapshot:** all 7 surfaces render on test PDP / cart / post-purchase / Klaviyo-test-send / Postscript-test-send with personalized rec (not "no recommendations" empty state).

### Step 3 — Wire the loyalty-tier-segment-overlay (Weeks 3–4, ~3 hr)

Per Smile.io (Move #8) tier data:

1. Sync Smile.io tier (`Insider` / `VIP` / `Icon`) into the rec engine as a metadata layer
2. Set tier-weighted rec ranking: VIP-tier-customer sees higher-AOV / lower-discount-leaning recs; Insider sees entry-point-leaning recs
3. Build per-tier AOV-target band: VIP AOV target $80; Insider AOV target $50

**Verification snapshot:** a fake VIP customer sees an AOV $80+ recommendation; a fake Insider sees an AOV $50 rec; log shows the tier-metadata is read on every rec request.

### Step 4 — Wire the SMS-cohort-LTV-band-routing (Weeks 4–5, ~2 hr)

Per Triple Whale (Move #6) SMS-cohort-LTV data:

1. Sync Triple Whale cohort LTV bands (high-LTV / mid-LTV / low-LTV) into the rec engine
2. Set cohort-weighted rec ranking: high-LTV cohort sees "complete the look" / VIP-only-product recs; low-LTV cohort sees "frequently bought together" / value-bundle recs
3. Tag every rec-engaged customer with `rec_engaged: true` for Move #6 cohort overlay

**Verification snapshot:** Triple Whale shows `rec_engaged` cohort has 10–25% higher 30-day LTV vs non-rec-engaged cohort (the canonical "killer test" for Move #20.2).

### Step 5 — Wire the Move #2 + Move #11 + Move #14 cross-pollination (Weeks 5–6, ~3 hr)

1. **Move #2 cross-pollination:** ReConvert post-purchase cross-sell now uses rec-engine feed (instead of static "complete the set")
2. **Move #11 cross-pollination:** Recharge replenishment reminder email/SMS now uses rec-engine "subscribe to related consumable" block
3. **Move #14 cross-pollination:** Lifecycle flows (winback / sunset / birthday / loyalty tier-up) get a "you might also like" email block via Klaviyo flow integration

**Verification snapshot:** every Move #14 flow shows the rec-engine feed (not "no recommendations"); AOV per cross-pollinated flow is 8–15% higher than the pre-Move-#20.2 baseline.

### Step 6 — A/B test the rec engine vs static baseline (Weeks 6–8, ~4 hr)

Per Move #9.5 PDP A/B testing program (already shipped):

1. Run an A/B test: rec-engine feed vs static "frequently bought together" on the PDP for 14 days
2. Compute winner via `scripts/pdp_ab_test.py` (existing Playbook 09.5): CVR-lift + AOV-lift + cohort-LTV-overlay
3. Promote winner to 100% on PDP + cart + post-purchase surfaces
4. Run a follow-up SMS A/B test: rec-engine SMS vs generic "shop new arrivals" SMS for 14 days; promote winner

**Verification snapshot:** A/B test returns a winner (rec-engine ≥ +5% CVR OR ≥ +8% AOV vs static); promoted to 100% on all surfaces; cohort-LTV-overlay check passes (rec-cohort LTV within ±10% of non-rec-cohort, NOT a high-CTR-low-LTV trap from Move #10 Pitfall #15).

### Step 7 — Brand-voice product-description iteration (Weeks 8–10, ~3 hr)

Per Move #20 generative-AI-engine Pillar 3 (research/16):

1. Use Jasper + Typeface + brand-voice-Wired to generate per-product rec-card copy in 5 voice variants (Default / Luxury / Sustainable / Gen-Z / B2B)
2. Replace generic "recommended for you" copy with voice-anchored rec copy
3. Sync the brand-voice cohort-tag into Triple Whale for Move #20 LTV-overlay math

**Verification snapshot:** every rec card on every surface has voice-anchored copy (not generic); brand-voice-cohort shows 5–15% higher rec-CTR vs no-voice-cohort.

### Step 8 — Quarterly cadence + KPI dashboard (Weeks 10–14, ~2 hr)

Recurring:

1. **Weekly:** review rec-engaged-cohort LTV vs non-rec-engaged cohort LTV in Triple Whale (the canonical "killer test")
2. **Monthly:** review per-surface CVR + AOV + email-CTR + SMS-CTR for all 7 surfaces
3. **Quarterly:** re-train rec engine on new order data (Rebuy: automated; Nosto: manual refresh; Dynamic Yield: ML-retrain-on-pipeline)
4. **Quarterly:** brand-voice-iteration-cycle (re-prompt Jasper + Typeface on new product copy)

**Verification snapshot:** dashboard at `dashboards/ai-product-rec-feed-health.html` (or equivalent) shows 4 KPIs (rec-engaged-cohort-LTV / per-surface CVR / per-surface AOV / AI-rec-cohort-overlap-with-Move-8-loyalty-tier) — all in target band.

## Common pitfalls (15 from real builds)

1. **Installing Rec engine without Triple Whale cohort merge** — you can't measure rec-cohort LTV without it. **Fix:** verify Move #6 fully shipped (all 7 gates pass) BEFORE installing; enable `rec_engaged: true` cohort tag on Day 1.
2. **Installing Rec engine without Move #2 post-purchase-upsell shipped** — Move #20.2 compounds Move #2's 8–12% AOV lift into 12–20%; without Move #2 baseline you can't measure compound ROI. **Fix:** ship Move #2 first (1 day); then ship Move #20.2.
3. **Single-surface deployment** (rec engine on PDP only) — captures only 30–40% of structural upside. **Fix:** ship all 7 surfaces in the Week 2–3 sprint; don't partial-ship.
4. **No loyalty-tier-segment-overlay** — recs are tier-blind; VIPs see Insider-leverage recs and vice versa. **Fix:** wire Smile.io tier into the rec engine in Step 3; tier-weighted ranking is required.
5. **No SMS-cohort-LTV-band-routing** — recs are LTV-band-blind; high-LTV cohort sees low-LTV-cohort recs. **Fix:** wire Triple Whale LTV bands into the rec engine in Step 4; cohort-weighted ranking is required.
6. **Using static "frequently bought together" instead of the rec engine** — caps at 5–8% AOV lift vs 8–15% with ML. **Fix:** Path B DEFAULT uses Rebuy Pro OR Nosto Growth's ML engine; the static-only path is the legacy Rebuy Starter / Nosto Starter tier.
7. **Rec engine on PDP but no rec engine on cart drawer / post-purchase** — captures only 50% of the structural AOV-lift upside. **Fix:** ship the 7-surface stack in Step 2; the cart-drawer + post-purchase surfaces carry 40–50% of the AOV lift per Rebuy 2024 benchmarks.
8. **No brand-voice product-description iteration** — rec cards have generic copy; brand voice erodes 5–15% CTR. **Fix:** Step 7 brand-voice-iteration with Jasper + Typeface; re-prompt quarterly.
9. **A/B testing the rec engine vs static without cohort-LTV overlay** — high-CTR-low-LTV trap from Move #10 Pitfall #15; promotes winners that look good but tank 12-month LTV. **Fix:** Step 6 cohort-LTV-overlay check requires rec-engaged-cohort-LTV within ±10% of non-rec-engaged-cohort-LTV; this is non-negotiable.
10. **Stopping the A/B test at first significant result** — inflates false-positive rate to 15–25% per Move #9.5 Pitfall #7. **Fix:** require a 14-day window with minimum 2,500 conversions per arm; `scripts/pdp_ab_test.py` enforces this.
11. **No rec-engaged cohort tag in Triple Whale** — you lose the canonical cohort-LTV-overlay layer. **Fix:** Step 1 wires `rec_engaged: true`; Triple Whale shows the cohort delta in Move #6 dashboard.
12. **Choosing Dynamic Yield at Path A GMV** — over-engineering; $1k+/mo cost eats the ROI. **Fix:** Path A brands default to Rebuy Starter or Nosto Starter; Path C brands use Dynamic Yield.
13. **Rec engine without customer-data injection from Klaviyo** — recommendations are session-only; cross-session recs fail. **Fix:** Step 1 wires Klaviyo `identified` state; rec engine uses Klaviyo segment + customer-attribute as input.
14. **Rec engine without loyalty-tier data from Smile.io** — VIP-only-product recs are impossible. **Fix:** Step 3 wires Smile.io tier metadata; VIP-only-product rec requires `tier === 'VIP'` filter.
15. **No quarterly cadence + KPI dashboard** — rec engine performance drifts 20–40% over 6 months without re-training. **Fix:** Step 8 quarterly cadence: re-train, re-prompt, re-A/B test, re-tag cohort.
