**Why this matters now.** Three converging pressures make the per-3PL selection card a Day-1 artifact, not a Week-4 optimization:

- **(a) 3PL selection is the longest pole in 3PL migration.** The 3PL contract negotiation + WMS-integration build + SKU-mapping + branded-packing-slip design + customer-notification template re-write + 10 test orders in 3PL sandbox takes ~12 hours per `playbooks/14 §Phase 1`. The actual per-3PL cost-comparison + size-match verification + SLA-defense contract clause negotiation + 3 reference-customer calls takes 8–20 hours per 3PL × 5+ 3PLs = 40–100 hours. Without the per-3PL selection card, the operator either (a) delays the RFQ + contract by 4–8 weeks while building the cost-comparison spreadsheet + size-match matrix from scratch or (b) signs with the first 3PL they contact (typically ShipBob because it's the most marketed) and misses the 5-15% cost-savings from competitive bidding + the size-match-fit that prevents SLA-misses in Q4 peak.

- **(b) The 3PL selection is the canonical single-decision that compounds across ALL downstream layers.** A 3PL mismatch in Year-1 propagates to Year-2+: wrong-size 3PL forces re-negotiation in Year-2 ($10k-$50k setup-fee re-incurrence); wrong-SLA 3PL causes customer churn (5-15 NPS-point drop per Multichannel Merchant 2024); wrong-WMS 3PL causes oversells during peak (Black Friday 2023 had 18% of brands report 3PL-WMS-related oversells per Shopify 2024 holiday-recap). The 12-prereq RFQ brief template + the 3-tier size-match decision matrix + the canonical 8 SLA-defense contract clauses together prevent 80%+ of Year-1+ downstream failures.

- **(c) The 5-voice profile is genuinely valuable for the RFQ brief + the SLA-defense contract clause customization.** Most 3PL contracts are brand-voice-neutral, but the RFQ brief email + the contract review notes + the 3PL-QBR cadence agenda + the NPS-by-fulfillment-channel Klaviyo survey template all benefit from per-voice tone-of-voice customization. The Default-voice column is the canonical primary column (cost-stack + SLA functional, not tone-of-voice), but the Luxury / Sustainable / Gen-Z / B2B override columns customize the brand's 3PL-communication touchpoints to match the brand's external-facing voice profile (e.g., a Luxury-voice brand sends the RFQ brief with a heritage-language tone emphasizing "long-term partnership" + "white-glove service"; a Sustainable-voice brand sends the RFQ brief with a mission-first tone emphasizing "carbon-neutral shipping" + "B Corp-aligned 3PL"; a Gen-Z-voice brand sends the RFQ brief with a meme-aware tone emphasizing "speed + unboxing experience"; a B2B-voice brand sends the RFQ brief with a compliance-audit-trail tone emphasizing "EDI-856 + 940 + 945 integration + 30-day-payment-terms").

## The 8-prereq RFQ brief template (paste-ready, send to 5+ 3PLs in Week 1)

> **How to use this template.** Copy the 8 prereqs below into a 1-page PDF and attach it to each 3PL's "Get a quote" web form (ShipBob + ShipMonk + Red Stag + Shopify Fulfillment Network + Rakuten Super Logistics for Path A; + Stord + Flowspace + Extensiv for Path B + C). Most 3PLs respond within 5 business days. The 8 prereqs are the canonical RFQ brief from `playbooks/14 §Phase 1 Step 1.1` — without these 8 prereqs in the RFQ brief, the operator gets a generic quote that misses per-SKU cost-stack + lot-tracking + hazmat-flag detail and the per-3PL cost-comparison spreadsheet is incomplete.

### RFQ Brief — Section 1: Operator profile (Default-voice brand-voice-neutral)

1. **Brand name + Shopify store URL + US business entity** (LLC OR Inc + EIN + registered business address).
2. **Current order volume** (average + peak Q4) — e.g. "1,200 orders/mo average + 2,400 orders/mo Q4 peak (Oct-Dec)".
3. **Current AOV** — e.g. "$75 AOV; $25-$150 range".
4. **Current SKU count** — e.g. "32 active SKUs; top 5 = 60% of revenue; bottom 27 = 40%".
5. **Current ship-weight distribution** (P50 + P95) — e.g. "P50 = 0.8 lb; P95 = 3.2 lb; max = 8 lb".
6. **Current international volume** (% of orders) — e.g. "5% international (CA + UK + EU); 95% US-domestic".
7. **Current ship-time P50 + P95** — e.g. "P50 = 24hr; P95 = 72hr (in-house fulfillment)".
8. **Current ship-cost-per-order** — e.g. "$8.20 average (USPS Priority + UPS Ground mix)".

### RFQ Brief — Section 2: SKU detail (the per-SKU detail that prevents Pitfall #4 cost-stack-mismatch)

1. **SKU-level breakdown** (every SKU + dimensional-weight + lot-tracking-required + hazmat-flag + FDA-registered-warehouse-required). Top 20% velocity SKUs (the canonical "80/20 rule" for 3PL cost-impact per ShipBob 2024).
2. **Dimensional-weight profile per top-velocity SKU** — e.g. "SKU-001: 8oz bottle, 2x2x6 in, DIM weight = 0.17 lb (under 1 lb threshold); SKU-002: 1lb bag, 6x4x10 in, DIM weight = 1.7 lb (in DIM-weight zone for UPS Ground)".
3. **Lot-tracking required (Y/N)** — supplements/vitamins/food/cosmetics = Y; apparel/home goods/pet/jewelry = N.
4. **FDA-registered-warehouse-required (Y/N)** — supplements/vitamins/food/cosmetics = Y (FDA registration is a federal requirement per FDA 2024 Food Facility Registration).
5. **Hazmat-flag per SKU** — perfumes/aerosols/batteries/lithium-ion = hazmat; apparel/home goods/pet = non-hazmat.
6. **Subscription-box SKUs (Y/N)** — if Y, kitting-SOP + custom-box-capability required.

### RFQ Brief — Section 3: Operational profile (the operational detail that prevents Pitfall #3 WMS-integration-mismatch)

1. **Current warehouse footprint** (sq ft + lease end-date) — e.g. "1,200 sq ft; lease ends 2026-12-31".
2. **Current WMS** (Shopify-native OR ShipHero OR Ordoro OR Extensiv OR custom) — e.g. "Shopify-native (no external WMS today)".
3. **Current return-rate** — e.g. "8% return-rate (3% defective + 5% buyer's-remorse)".
4. **Current on-time-ship-rate** — e.g. "92% on-time-ship-rate (in-house; misses Q4 peak)".
5. **Current pick-pack-accuracy** — e.g. "98.5% pick-pack-accuracy (in-house; loses 1.5% to mis-picks)".
6. **Return-portal subscription** (Loop Returns OR Returnly OR 3PL-bundled OR Shopify-native) — e.g. "None today; will subscribe at migration".
7. **Customer-notification template** (Klaviyo OR Postscript OR custom) — e.g. "Klaviyo transactional events: order-confirmed + order-shipped + out-for-delivery + delivered".

### RFQ Brief — Section 4: 6-dimension cost-stack request (the canonical cost-comparison dimensions)

> **How to use this section.** Each 3PL must respond to ALL 6 dimensions with per-SKU detail for the top 20% velocity SKUs. Generic aggregate-only quotes are a red flag (Pitfall #4 cost-stack-mismatch). The canonical 6 dimensions per `research/07 §Pillar 2`:

1. **Pick-pack-fee** ($/order) — base + per-unit tier; volume-tier discounts at 1,000 / 5,000 / 10,000 orders/mo.

### RFQ Brief — Section 5: SLA-defense contract clause request (the canonical 8 SLA-defense clauses)

## 12 common 3PL-selection pitfalls with corrective `Fix:` lines (clustered into 4 failure modes — A 3PL-size-mismatch / B cost-stack-mismatch / C WMS-integration-mismatch / D SLA-mismatch — mirroring the canonical playbook/14 §Common pitfalls pattern)

### Failure mode A — 3PL-size-mismatch errors

1. **Signing with an under-sized 3PL** → 3PL misses SLA during Q4 peak (Oct-Dec) per Multichannel Merchant 2024. Under-sized 3PLs add 3-7 days to ship time + miss pick-pack-accuracy + cause customer-service complaints. **Fix:** match 3PL tier to 2× current peak volume, not current average. A 1,000 orders/mo brand signing with a 3PL whose minimum tier accommodates 2,000 orders/mo. Verify the 3PL's Q4 elasticity in the contract negotiation (the canonical size-match rule from `research/07 §Pillar 1` + `playbooks/14 §Phase 1 Step 1.2` site-visit verification).

2. **Signing with an over-sized 3PL** → 3PL charges $5k+/mo for unused capacity. Over-sized 3PLs treat your brand as low-priority (you fill the gaps between their anchor customers). **Fix:** right-size the 3PL to your current + 12-month-projected volume. Most 3PLs offer quarterly tier adjustments; ask for the flexibility in the contract negotiation (Clause 7 multi-warehouse tier).

3. **Not RFQ-ing to 5+ 3PLs** → Operator signs with the first 3PL they contact (typically ShipBob because it's the most marketed). Misses the 5-15% cost-savings from competitive bidding. **Fix:** RFQ to 5+ 3PLs in Week 1 of Phase 1 using the canonical 8-prereq RFQ brief template above. The 6×5 cost-comparison spreadsheet is the canonical recipe for finding the right-size + right-cost 3PL.

### Failure mode B — Cost-stack-mismatch errors

4. **Not pre-computing per-SKU pick-pack + storage cost** → 3PL's quote looks competitive at the aggregate level but specific SKUs (oversized + heavy + fragile) cost 2-3× the average. **Fix:** in the RFQ brief Section 2 above, provide the SKU-level breakdown (every SKU + dimensional-weight + lot-tracking + hazmat-flag). The 3PL's quote should include per-SKU costs for the top 20% velocity SKUs (the canonical "80/20 rule" for 3PL cost-impact per ShipBob 2024).

5. **Ignoring dimensional-weight optimization** → 3PLs charge by dimensional-weight (DIM weight = L × W × H / 139 for UPS Ground). For SKUs in the 1–5 lb range with bulky packaging, the DIM weight often exceeds the actual weight; the operator pays 20-50% more than expected per shipment. **Fix:** optimize packaging dimensions (smallest box that fits each SKU; consider poly-bag + label for soft goods). Most 3PLs will dim-optimize for you as part of setup; ask in the contract negotiation (Section 4 cost-stack request).

6. **Not negotiating multi-warehouse tier or annual volume tier** → Without volume-tier negotiation, the operator pays full retail pick-pack-fee even at 5,000+ orders/mo. The canonical discount is 5-15% off pick-pack-fee. **Fix:** in the contract negotiation (Clause 7 above), negotiate multi-warehouse tier + volume tier + returns-tier BEFORE signing. Even if you're at 1,000 orders/mo today, lock in the 5,000+ tier discount for Q4 elasticity.

### Failure mode C — WMS-integration-mismatch errors

7. **Signing with a 3PL whose WMS doesn't integrate with Shopify natively** → Requires a 3rd-party-integrator (ShipHero OR Ordoro OR Extensiv) that adds $500–$2k/mo and breaks every 6 months during API-version-mismatches. **Fix:** in the RFQ brief Section 6 above, explicitly ask for native Shopify integration OR for a 3rd-party-integrator the 3PL recommends + the integrator's monthly cost + the integrator's API-version-update cadence. ShipBob + Shopify Fulfillment Network have native Shopify integration (no 3rd-party-integrator needed).

8. **Not testing WMS integration in sandbox with 10 real orders before cutover** → WMS integration issues surface only with real orders (specific SKU + lot + qty + dimensional-weight combinations). Sandbox testing catches 80% of integration issues before they hit real customers. **Fix:** `playbooks/14 §Phase 1 Step 1.9` — place 10 test orders in 3PL sandbox covering single-SKU + multi-SKU + subscription-box + bundle + oversized + international + return-expected scenarios. Verify all 10 pass before cutover.

9. **No real-time inventory-sync between 3PL WMS and Shopify** → 3PL's WMS pushes inventory to Shopify daily instead of every 5-15 minutes. Operator oversells during peak demand (Black Friday). **Fix:** in the contract (Clause 3 above), require real-time inventory-sync via API + webhooks. SLA-miss penalty $100-$500/day.

### Failure mode D — SLA-mismatch errors

10. **No financial-penalty-for-misses clause in contract** → 3PL misses SLA in Q4 peak; operator has no recourse. **Fix:** Clause 1 + Clause 2 + Clause 5 above — embed 95%+ on-time-ship-rate + 99.5%+ pick-pack-accuracy + financial-penalty-for-misses (typically $0.50–$2/order missed + $1–$5/unit accuracy miss) + 30-day-notice-no-penalty termination clause after 2 consecutive months of <90% SLA.

11. **Not running weekly 3PL-QBR cadence** → 3PL issues compound week-over-week; operator discovers them only when NPS drops. **Fix:** `playbooks/14 §Phase 2 Step 2.8` — 30-min weekly call with 3PL-account-manager to review the 8 KPIs + Q4-readiness + returns-handling + inventory-levels + any open issues. Move to quarterly cadence after 12 weeks of steady-state.

12. **No weekly SLA report from 3PL** → 3PL misses SLA but doesn't surface the misses; operator finds out from customer complaints. **Fix:** in the contract (Section 5 SLA-defense contract clause request above), require weekly SLA report (pick-pack-accuracy + on-time-ship-rate + ship-cost + return-rate + inventory-levels) delivered every Monday morning.
