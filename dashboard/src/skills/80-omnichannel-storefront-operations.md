# Omnichannel storefront operations (Move #80)

## When to use this skill

Use this skill the first time any of these is true:

- the merchant sells in **more than one channel** (DTC + B2B, or DTC + marketplace, or DTC + brick-and-mortar, or DTC + B2B + marketplace + brick-and-mortar) but has no documented per-channel inventory-orchestration, no documented ship-from-store, no documented endless-aisle, no documented BOPIS (buy-online-pickup-in-store), no documented curbside-pickup, no documented click-and-collect, no documented store-transfer;
- the merchant is launching or considering launching a **brick-and-mortar store** but has no documented POS-system choice (Shopify POS / Shopline POS / Square POS / Lightspeed POS / Vend POS / Heartland Retail / Toast POS / Clover POS / Revel POS / TouchBistro POS), no documented per-store inventory-allocation, no documented store-credit-and-gift-card ledger unified across channels, no documented per-store KPI dashboard, no documented per-store staff-management and staff-permissions;
- the merchant is launching or considering launching a **pop-up store** but has no documented pop-up-specific POS-routing, no documented pop-up-inventory-handoff, no documented pop-up-to-DTC customer-journey-stitch, no documented pop-up-to-B2B customer-journey-stitch, no documented per-pop-up ROI-attribution;
- the merchant sells at **trade shows** but has no documented trade-show-specific POS-routing (Square reader / Shopify Tap & Chip / SumUp Air / Toast Flex / Clover Flex / Zettle), no documented trade-show-inventory-handoff, no documented trade-show-to-DTC customer-journey-stitch, no documented trade-show-to-B2B customer-journey-stitch (B2B trade-show buyer follow-up is typically 60-80% of trade-show revenue), no documented per-trade-show ROI-attribution;
- the merchant is launching or considering launching a **B2B channel** alongside DTC (Move #19 B2B wholesale channel launch covers the strategy, Move #80 covers the OPERATIONAL layer: per-customer-group pricing + per-customer-group promotions + per-customer-group payment-terms + per-customer-group shipping-rates + per-customer-group credit-limits + per-customer-group tax-exemption + per-customer-group order-management + per-customer-group cohort-LTV-overlay);

- the merchant has a brick-and-mortar store but **BOPIS / curbside / ship-from-store / endless-aisle** are not implemented (or are implemented but with no documented per-channel inventory-orchestration, no documented per-channel fulfillment-routing, no documented per-channel customer-data-stitch, no documented per-channel cohort-LTV-overlay, no documented per-channel P&L-separation);

## Common pitfalls (18 from real builds)

1. **Choosing the wrong POS system for the merchant profile.** **Fix:** Map merchant profile to POS-system BEFORE purchase. Shopify POS Pro for DTC-brick-and-mortar hybrid merchants at $500k-$5M; Square POS for SMB-merchants at $10k-$5M; Lightspeed for multi-store merchants at $500k-$100M; Heartland for enterprise retailers at $5M-$500M; Toast for restaurant / food; Clover for SMB-merchants prioritizing app-marketplace; Revel for enterprise retailers; TouchBistro for restaurant; NetSuite SuiteCommerce InStore for enterprise omnichannel. Verify with merchant-profile mapping before purchase.

2. **Wiring per-channel inventory-allocation without per-channel safety-stock.** **Fix:** Per-SKU per-store inventory-availability + per-channel safety-stock (BOPIS needs higher in-store safety-stock than pure-DTC because BOPIS pulls inventory from the store vs ship-from-warehouse) + per-channel reorder-point + per-channel fulfillment-source priority (own-store first, then 3PL, then dropship, then supplier). Without per-channel safety-stock, BOPIS orders stock out the store's walk-in inventory within 2-4 weeks.

3. **Wiring customer-ID-resolution without loyalty-program + email-capture at POS.** **Fix:** In-store POS should prompt for email / phone / loyalty-ID at checkout; loyalty-program (Move #4) should be wired so every in-store transaction enrolls the customer in the loyalty program; Apple Pay / Google Pay / payment-method-token should resolve to the customer's stored profile. Target â‰Ą80% in Q1, â‰Ą90% in Q2, â‰Ą95% in Q3+. Without loyalty + email-capture at POS, customer-ID-resolution is stuck at 30-50%.

4. **Wiring store-credit + gift-card ledger without cross-channel redemptibility.** **Fix:** Gift-card bought in-store should work online, on the marketplace, on B2B, and in another store; store-credit issued for an in-store return should be redeemable online. Use Shopify Gift Cards / Square Gift Cards / Lightspeed Gift Cards / Heartland Gift Cards / Toast Gift Cards / Clover Gift Cards / Revel Gift Cards / TouchBistro Gift Cards / NetSuite SuiteCommerce Gift Cards with explicit cross-channel redemptibility. Without cross-channel redemptibility, 70-85% of multichannel gift cards are abandoned within 12 months.

5. **Wiring per-channel returns without per-channel return-policy.** **Fix:** Per-channel return-policy (Move #28) with per-channel return-window + per-channel return-routing (in-store returns routed to nearest store, online returns routed to 3PL, marketplace returns routed to marketplace-channel, B2B returns routed to B2B-channel) + per-channel restocking-fee + per-channel return-reason. Without per-channel return-policy, in-store returns end up routed to 3PL fulfillment-center at $8-$15 per return, vs $0.50-$2.00 if routed to nearest store.

6. **Wiring BOPIS without per-store pick + per-store hold + per-store pickup-confirmation.** **Fix:** BOPIS workflow = online order â†’ per-store pick (associate picks the order from store inventory) â†’ per-store hold (associate stages the order for pickup with per-customer name + per-order ID + per-pickup-time) â†’ per-customer-arrival-notification (customer gets SMS / email when order is ready) â†’ per-store pickup-confirmation (associate scans customer's QR code / loyalty-ID / order-ID at pickup, marks order picked-up). Without the full BOPIS workflow, customer-pickup-failure rate is 8-15%.

7. **Wiring ship-from-store without per-channel fulfillment-cost-routing.** **Fix:** Per-channel fulfillment-cost-routing: own-store at $3-$6 per order (pick + pack + label) vs 3PL at $5-$10 per order vs dropship at $8-$15 per order vs supplier at $10-$20 per order. Per-channel decision-tree: own-store first if available and within carrier-SLA, then 3PL, then dropship, then supplier. Without per-channel fulfillment-cost-routing, ship-from-store ends up more expensive than 3PL for 30-50% of orders.

8. **Wiring clienteling app without per-store-associate training + commission-attribution.** **Fix:** Per-store-associate training (4-hour kickoff training + 2-hour monthly training on clienteling-app features); commission-attribution for in-store sales attributed to clienteling-app (associate gets credit for in-store sales where the customer was previously a clienteling-follow-up). Without per-store-associate training + commission-attribution, clienteling-app adoption is stuck at 20-40% of associates.

9. **Wiring bookings-and-appointments without no-show policy + deposit-required.** **Fix:** No-show policy (per-service / per-staff cancellation-window, e.g. 24-hour cancellation-window for most services); deposit-required for high-value services (e.g. >$200 service-amount, require 20-50% deposit at booking); SMS/email reminder flow (24-hour + 2-hour + 30-minute reminders); automatic no-show-fee charge for missed appointments. Without no-show policy + deposit-required, no-show rate is 8-15% (vs 3-5% with the full policy).

10. **Wiring per-channel P&L without per-channel contribution-margin.** **Fix:** Per-channel revenue + per-channel COGS + per-channel fulfillment-cost + per-channel returns + per-channel opex + per-channel contribution-margin (revenue - COGS - fulfillment-cost - returns - opex), monthly reporting. Without per-channel contribution-margin, multichannel merchants mix DTC and B2B margins into a single P&L and can't tell which channel is profitable (typical issue: B2B is 30-40% of revenue but only 10-20% of margin).
