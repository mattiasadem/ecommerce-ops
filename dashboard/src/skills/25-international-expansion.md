> Move #22 is the canonical **cross-border DTC expansion** move for US-based Shopify-DTC brands at **$1M–$50M GMV** ready to compound their US performance with revenue from **Canada + UK + EU + Australia + Japan + DACH + Nordics** via the **Shopify Markets + EU IOSS + UK VAT MOSS + AU GST + JP APPI** regulatory substrate and the **Klarna + AfterPay + iDEAL + Konbini + Alipay** localized payment-method stack — capturing the **30–150% Year-1 international revenue lift** at **25:1 default Year-1 ROI** ($2.5M median incremental international revenue / $100k median Year-1 cost stack on a $5M US DTC base) per Shopify Markets 2024 + EU IOSS 2024 + UK VAT MOSS 2024 + AU GST 2024 + JP APPI 2024 + Klarna 2024 + AfterPay 2024 + iDEAL 2024 + Konbini 2024 + Stripe International 2024 + PayPal International 2024 + Zonos 2024 + TaxJar International 2024 + Avalara Cross-Border 2024 + Weglot 2024 + ShipBob International 2024 + DHL Express 2024 + FedEx International 2024 + USPS International 2024 + GDPR 2024 + UK GDPR ICO 2024 + EU Green Claims Directive 2026 + Canada USMCA 2024 + Baymard International 2024 + BigCommerce International Benchmarks 2024 + Statista Cross-Border 2024 + Klaviyo Per-Locale 2024 + Postscript Per-Country 2024 + Gorgias Multilingual 2024 + Smile Multi-Currency 2024 + Triple Whale Per-Market FX 2024 + EU Customer Rights Directive 2024 benchmarks. ## When to use this skill

**Use this skill when the operator has shipped the canonical US-centric Move #1 (cart-abandon) + Move #4 (welcome) + Move #6 (Triple Whale attribution) + Move #7 (SMS welcome) + Move #8 (loyalty) on the US substrate ≥90 days AND is at $1M+ US DTC GMV with strong US unit economics (LTV:CAC ≥3:1, contribution margin ≥30%) AND has documented international-demand signals (UK/EU/AU site-traffic >10% OR brand-keyword search-volume in target markets OR inbound wholesale-inquiries from international distributors).** Move #22 is structurally distinct from Move #13 (marketplace-expansion) + Move #14.5 (B2B-wholesale-launch) — it is the **DTC-direct cross-border** layer that compounds the canonical US stack into 4–7 international markets via Shopify Markets + EU IOSS + UK VAT MOSS + AU GST + the Klarna/AfterPay/iDEAL/Konbini/Alipay payment stack. The operator should only initiate it when ALL of the following are true:

- **Shopify store live ≥12 months + ≥$1M US DTC GMV for 3 consecutive months (Path B/C) OR ≥$100k (Path A only).** Move #22 is the cross-border expansion move for brands that have already shipped the canonical US-centric 30-day-rollout MVP AND have ≥12 months of US order history + baseline unit economics (CAC, LTV, AOV, contribution-margin). International expansion eats 5–15pp of contribution margin via duties + shipping subsidies + FX fees; a brand with <30% US contribution-margin should defer international expansion until US unit-economics improve. Below $100k US GMV (Path A scope), CA-only expansion is justified for testing cross-border mechanics; UK + EU + AU are unjustified ops overhead. Verify: Shopify Analytics → Reports → Gross-sales-by-month returning ≥$8.3k/mo for 3 consecutive months ($100k GMV Path A) OR ≥$83k/mo ($1M GMV Path B/C).
- **US LTV:CAC ≥3:1 AND US contribution-margin ≥30%.** International expansion is a multiplier on existing US ops, not a replacement. If US LTV:CAC is <3:1, international burns cash 2–3× faster. Per research/04 Pillar 4, brands with <30% US contribution-margin cap at <15% international contribution-margin after duties + shipping subsidies + FX fees + 3PL costs → not viable. Verify: Triple Whale → Reports → Cohort LTV (ON vs OFF) returning ≥3:1 AND Shopify Analytics → Reports → Gross-profit-by-month returning ≥30% margin.
- **Active Move #1 (cart-abandon-flow) + Move #4 (welcome-series) + Move #7 (SMS welcome+cart) + Move #8 (loyalty) shipped ≥90 days with measurable revenue.** The 4 flows are the canonical international-growth-engine (cart-abandon + welcome + SMS + loyalty all reuse the Klaviyo + Postscript + Smile wiring that any new international subscriber inherits automatically). A brand without the 4 flows gets <30% of the international LTV upside; the per-market welcome + cart-abandon + loyalty flows all compound the US substrate. Verify: Klaviyo flow `cart_abandon_v1` + `welcome_v1` shipped ≥90 days with ≥$2k revenue/mo from each; Postscript flow `sms_welcome_v1` + `sms_cart_abandon_v1` shipped ≥60 days with ≥$1k revenue/mo; Smile.io installed with points + tiers configured.
- **Triple Whale (or Polar) attribution shipped ≥90 days with cohort-LTV-baseline.** Per-market revenue attribution requires the cohort-LTV-baseline as reference frame; without it, the operator cannot detect Phase-vs-US regressions caused by cross-border shipping-time or currency-display. Triple Whale's per-market FX conversion is automatic; Polar supports per-market via custom dimensions. Verify: Triple Whale installed ≥90 days + cohort-LTV-dtc-baseline showing ≥$120 LTV per the Move #1 + #4 + #7 + #8 cohort.
- **Documented voice framework with ≥5 voice profiles (Default + Luxury + Sustainable + Gen-Z + B2B).** International expansion needs at minimum UK-English + EU-localized variants of each voice profile. A brand with only US-English Default-voice cannot launch UK without a 1-week UK-English pass; cannot launch EU without EU-Sustainable per EU-Green-Claims-Directive-2026 (no "eco-friendly" without certification). Verify: Asset 02 brand-voice-document containing ≥5 voice profiles; UK-English pass complete; EU-Sustainable-variant complete.
- **Documented international-demand signals.** International expansion without demand signals is unjustified ops overhead. Verify AT LEAST ONE of: (a) UK/EU/AU/JP site-traffic ≥10% of total site-traffic in last 90 days (Shopify Analytics → Reports → Sessions-by-country), OR (b) brand-keyword search-volume in target markets (Google Trends, SEMrush, Ahrefs), OR (c) inbound wholesale inquiries from international distributors (Gorgias macro + inbox count), OR (d) US-baseline-LTV ≥2× US-AOV indicating AOV headroom for international price-uplift, OR (e) existing-customer-international-shipping-address rate ≥10% (Shopify Orders → Shipping-country breakdown).
- **Shopify Markets Pro annual fee ($2,400/yr per Shopify Markets pricing 2024) budgeted.** Markets Pro unlocks per-market pricing-psychology (price overrides per currency) + per-market SEO (per-market meta + landing-page) + per-market domains (country-specific TLDs / subdomains / subdirectories) + per-market payment-method routing (Klarna for DACH, AfterPay for UK/AU/CA, Konbini for JP). Free-tier Shopify Markets supports currency conversion + duties treatment but NOT per-market pricing-psychology OR per-market domains. Verify: Shopify Admin → Settings → Markets → Pro showing "Active" tier.
- **EU IOSS registration active (or in-progress with ≥2-week lead-time-before-launch).** EU IOSS is mandatory for cross-border EU shipments ≤€150; registration via the EU portal is free but takes 2–4 weeks. Brands launching EU without IOSS trigger customs holds on every shipment + 5–14 day delays + 30%+ customer complaints. Verify: EU IOSS portal → confirmation email on file with IOSS-trader-ID + start-date.
- **UK VAT MOSS registration active (or in-progress).** UK is no longer in EU; separate registration with HMRC required. For shipments ≤£135, the UK VAT MOSS scheme lets the brand charge VAT at checkout with no customs hold. Verify: HMRC → VAT MOSS confirmation on file with UK-VAT-MOSS-trader-ID + start-date.
- **AU GST registration active.** Australia requires GST registration at A$75k AU sales (~US$50k). Charge 10% GST at checkout. Customs duties 0–10% per HS code. Verify: ATO → ABN + GST registration confirmation on file.
- **Cross-border-friendly SKU profile.** Not too heavy (<2kg), not too fragile, not too regulated. Apparel + beauty + home + pet-food work; supplements + CBD + perishable foods require category-specific regulatory expertise beyond Move #22 scope. Verify: SKU-weight <2kg for ≥80% of catalog; SKU-fragile-rating acceptable for cross-border transit; HS-code-per-SKU documented.
- **4–8 hr/wk operator-time during Phase 1 (lower after launch) + $5k–$25k one-time setup budget.** Phase 1 market-launch is 25–40 hr one-time; Phase 2 EU + AU is 40–80 hr; Phase 3 JP + DACH + Nordics is 60–120 hr. Operator has committed 4–8 contiguous hours per week for the 6–12-month rollout. Verify: operator has committed 4–8 hr/wk + $5k–$25k one-time budget.
- **DMARC + GDPR + UK-GDPR + AU-Privacy-Act + JP-APPI compliance baseline + DPO-designate documented.** International expansion is the canonical moment when US-centric brands discover they need a Data-Protection-Officer (DPO) for EU + UK GDPR compliance. Per GDPR Article 37, a DPO is required for brands processing EU personal data at scale; the DPO can be a part-time consultant. Brands without DPO + GDPR-compliance-audit cap at €20M or 4%-of-revenue fines per violation. Verify: DPO-designate documented + GDPR-data-processing-agreement with Klaviyo + Gorgias + Shopify + Triple Whale + any 3PL on file.

**Don't use this skill when:**

- **<$100k US DTC GMV OR <$1M for Path B/C.** Below this threshold, international expansion is unjustified ops overhead. The 50–500 international orders/mo that CA generates at $100k US GMV is enough to test cross-border mechanics without committing to IOSS + EU translation + multi-market pricing; this is the Path A scope. Path B requires $1M+; Path C requires $10M+. Defer UK + EU + AU + JP + DACH + Nordics until US GMV justifies the per-market ops cost.
- **US LTV:CAC <3:1 OR US contribution-margin <30%.** Per research/04 Pillar 4 + cost-stack table, international eats 5–15pp of US contribution margin. A brand with <30% US margin caps at <15% international margin after duties + shipping subsidies + FX fees + 3PL costs → not viable. Fix US unit economics first.
- **Move #1 + Move #4 + Move #7 + Move #8 not shipped ≥90 days.** Without the 4 foundational flows, the per-market welcome + cart-abandon + SMS + loyalty flows have no canonical reference frame; international LTV upside caps at 30% of the post-launch steady-state per Triple Whale per-market FX benchmarks.
- **No Triple Whale or equivalent attribution ≥90 days.** Without the cohort-LTV-baseline, the operator cannot detect cross-border-induced regressions in the 5–15pp contribution-margin band; international ROI becomes unverifiable.
- **US-only supply-chain (US-only ingredients, US-only certifications, regulated-to-US-only-markets).** Cross-border brands need a supply-chain that can clear EU EFSA + UK FSA + AU FSANZ + JP MHLW regulatory markets. Supplements + CBD + cosmetics-with-EU-restricted-ingredients + alcohol + medical-devices need category-specific regulatory expertise.
- **No team / no operator / no creative agency bandwidth.** Move #22 is structurally distinct from Move #13 (marketplace) + Move #14.5 (B2B-wholesale) — it's a multi-market, multi-language, multi-payment-method, multi-regulatory stack that requires ≥0.5 FTE dedicated operator AND a creative-agency partner for per-market PDP imagery (especially DE/FR/ES/JP). Brands with solo-operator capacity <8 hr/wk should defer Move #22 until they can hire or outsource.
- **Handmade / one-of-a-kind / per-order-manufactured SKUs.** Cross-border marketplace penalties for late delivery + customs delays can drain 10–20% of contribution margin on per-order-manufactured SKUs that have 14–30 day production timelines. Move #22 assumes ≤48-hour pick-pack for cross-border.
- **No DPO designate + no GDPR-compliance-audit.** GDPR fines are €20M or 4%-of-revenue per violation; a brand launching EU without DPO + data-processing-agreements is taking on existential risk. Defer until DPO is designated.
- **Active promotional peak (BFCM / Black Friday / Mother's Day / Father's Day / Valentine's Day / Cyber Monday / launch-week).** International launch during a peak window risks customs delays during the highest-revenue period AND customer-service overload. Defer until the peak-window passes.

## The build (3 phases over 6–12 months)

### Phase 1 — Foundation: Canada + United Kingdom (Months 1–2)

**Goal:** Ship the 2 lowest-friction English-language markets. Verify the cross-border operations primitives (currency, duties, shipping, returns, CS) work end-to-end before adding EU/AU regulatory complexity.

**Step 1.2 — UK VAT MOSS registration (start Day 1, takes 2–4 weeks).** Register for UK VAT MOSS via HMRC portal (free; takes 2–4 weeks). The VAT MOSS scheme lets the brand charge VAT at checkout for shipments ≤£135 with no customs hold. For shipments >£135, customer pays VAT + duties on delivery (DAP). ## Common pitfalls (15 from real builds)

**Cluster A — Regulatory + duty pitfalls (5 pitfalls)**

1. **Launching EU without IOSS registration.** Customs holds on every shipment ≤€150, 5–14 day delays, 30%+ customer complaints. Fix: EU IOSS registration takes 2–4 weeks; START the process 8 weeks before EU launch (Phase 2 Step 2.1). Per EU IOSS 2024: IOSS is mandatory for cross-border EU shipments; non-registered traders face customs holds + per-shipment fees. EU IOSS-trader-ID + start-date must be on file before public launch.
2. **Confusing UK with EU post-Brexit.** Treating UK as EU market because English-language-similarity. UK VAT MOSS is SEPARATE from EU IOSS; UK has its own £135 threshold + separate HMRC registration; cross-border to UK from EU requires UK VAT registration post-Brexit. Fix: register UK VAT MOSS independently (HMRC portal) + treat UK as its own market with its own VAT/shipping/returns policies. Per UK VAT MOSS 2024 + UK Consumer Rights Act 2015.
3. **Using US-only HS-code for AU customs without AU GST registration.** AU GST registration is mandatory at A$75k AU sales (low threshold); un-registered traders face 10% GST penalty + import-duty-back-charge on every shipment. Fix: ATO → GST registration at Day 0 of Phase 2; charge 10% GST at checkout via Shopify Markets Pro; per-SKU HS-code audit per ATO schedule. Per AU GST 2024 + AU Consumer Law 2024.
4. **Forgetting GDPR + UK GDPR + JP APPI for international email + CS capture.** EU double-opt-in is mandatory for email + GDPR-compliant-cookie-consent + clear-consent-language + DPO designate + data-processing-agreements with Klaviyo + Gorgias + Shopify + Triple Whale. Per GDPR 2024 + UK GDPR ICO 2024 + JP APPI 2024: GDPR fines are €20M or 4%-of-revenue per violation. Per JP APPI: explicit consent for cross-border data-transfer. Fix: cookie-consent banner from Day 1 of Phase 1 + DPO designate documented + per-vendor DPAs on file.
5. **Confusing DDP-vs-DAP without per-market strategy.** DDP (seller-pays-duties) lifts CVR 15–25% per Shopify Markets 2024; DAP (customer-pays-duties-on-delivery) triggers 25–40% delivery-refusal rates. Brands that use DAP for ≤€150 EU shipments (where IOSS makes DDP essentially free) lose 15–25% CVR unnecessarily. Fix: DDP via IOSS/VAT MOSS for eligible shipments (EU ≤€150 + UK ≤£135 + CA under USMCA per-SKU duty-free + AU under AU$1,000 per HS-code); landed-cost-calculator (Zonos/TaxJar/Avalara) for non-eligible.

**Cluster B — Pricing + FX + translation pitfalls (5 pitfalls)**

6. **Using US prices (just currency-converted) for international markets.** $19.99 USD = €19.99 EUR (or €18.50 EUR, depending on FX rate at conversion moment). This drops CVR 20–40% per Shopify Markets benchmarks. Fix: PPP pricing per market (5–15% above US price to offset shipping subsidies + duties + FX fees + lower local CAC) + per-market price-test before full launch.
7. **Not budgeting for FX fees in the P&L.** Stripe International charges 1.5% on US cards + 1% on non-US cards (capped) + a $0.30 fixed fee on currency conversion; PayPal International charges 3–4%; Shopify Payments tiered rates apply. Brands without 2.5% FX-fee budget silently lose 1.5–3.5% of margin. Fix: budget 2.5% FX fee in the international P&L; negotiate multi-currency settlement with Stripe/PayPal; use Markets Pro per-market price-psychology overrides.
8. **Not localizing voice for UK English.** US-English "pants" reads as underwear; "fanny pack" reads as vagina; "hood (of a car)" doesn't exist in UK; "elevator" → "lift"; "trousers" not "pants"; "jumper" not "sweater"; "sidewalk" → "pavement"; "apartment" → "flat". Fix: UK-English pass on all PDP + Klaviyo + Gorgias macros; budget 1 week per Shopify Markets 2024 + UK Consumer Rights Act 2015.
9. **Translating with Google Translate / DeepL without human review.** Embarrassing mistranslations + regulatory non-compliance for "organic" / "natural" / "eco-friendly" claims per EU Green Claims Directive 2026 (unsubstantiated environmental claims prohibited). Fix: human translation for all PDP + Klaviyo flows + Gorgias macros (10,000 words × $0.15/word × 3 languages = $4,500); AI translation OK for cart/checkout (low-risk). Per Weglot 2024 + EU Green Claims Directive 2026.
10. **Forgetting per-market pricing-psychology research.** $19.99 USD should NOT be exactly €19.99 EUR (which feels arbitrary in EU markets). Round to €19.95 or €19.90. Similarly, £14.99 GBP is canonical UK; CAD$24.99 is canonical CA; A$29.95 AUD is canonical AU. Fix: 30 min per market on pricing-psychology-research before launch; per-market round-number-rule documented in Asset 13 international-pricing-card. Per Statista Cross-Border Ecommerce 2024.

**Cluster C — Fulfillment + shipping pitfalls (3 pitfalls)**

11. **Returning US-sized packaging internationally.** Dimensional-weight shipping costs 2–3× more than weight suggests; 40–60% of international shipping cost is dimensional weight, not product weight. Brands that ship international orders in US-palletized packaging double their freight cost. Fix: redesign packaging for international (smaller, lighter, poly-mailers for soft goods); use ShipBob International dimensional-weight-optimization + zone-skipping.
12. **Not setting up Klarna/AfterPay in DACH + AU markets.** 30–40% of DE/NL/SE/AU shoppers won't complete checkout without BNPL per Klarna 2024 + AfterPay 2024. Per Shopify Markets Pro benchmarks: DACH + AU markets without BNPL cap at 60–70% checkout-completion vs 80–90% with BNPL. Fix: Klarna integration is 1–2 days; Markets Pro supports it natively; the conversion lift is immediate.
13. **Using US-only returns policy.** 30–60 day return shipping costs $15–$40 per item + 14–30 day transit per ShipBob International 2024. Brands with US 60–90 day return window applied to international lose 10–20% of contribution margin to return shipping costs. Fix: 30-day return window for international orders + $5–$15 restocking fee + returnless-refund policy for sub-$30 items.

**Cluster D — Payment-method + klaviyo-per-locale pitfalls (2 pitfalls)**

14. **Not adapting Klaviyo flows to local time zones.** Sending Klaviyo welcome + cart-abandon + post-purchase flows at US-friendly hours (e.g. 9am Eastern) means EU + AU + JP customers receive emails at 3pm EU + 11pm JP. Drop CTR 20–40% per Klaviyo per-locale 2024. Fix: per-market send-time defaults in Klaviyo (Klaviyo supports per-locale timing in flow settings); test per-market send-time-performance in first 30 days + iterate.
15. **Forgetting EU double-opt-in for Klaviyo welcome flow.** GDPR requires explicit opt-in for EU email; Klaviyo's default welcome flow assumes single-opt-in (which is CAN-SPAM-compliant in US but NOT GDPR-compliant in EU). Brands that ship US welcome flows to EU lists generate GDPR violations + Klaviyo deliverability risks. Fix: enable double-opt-in for EU lists in Klaviyo Settings → Lists → EU → Double-Opt-In = ON; per-market welcome-flow-variants per market. Per GDPR 2024 + UK GDPR ICO 2024.

**Plus 3 workflow pitfalls unique to Move #22:**

- **Don't launch Phase 2 (EU + AU) before Phase 1 (CA + UK) reaches steady-state.** The Phase-1 → Phase-2 boundary is the canonical decision-point: skip it and you can't tell whether EU + AU launch is causing the cross-border regression OR whether Phase 1 wasn't actually steady-state. Phase 1 requires ≥90 days of post-launch data with per-market revenue + per-market attribution green before Phase 2 starts.
- **Don't launch Phase 3 (JP + DACH + Nordics) before international revenue ≥20% of total revenue AND operator capacity supports.** Phase 3 is the highest-friction phase (Konbini + Klarna extension + JP APPI + in-region 3PL + per-market native-language voice); launching it on Phase 1+2 that hasn't reached 20% means you're paying the highest per-market ops cost at the lowest absolute-revenue-volume.
- **Don't ship per-market Klaviyo flows without per-locale + per-time-zone send-time defaults.** US-friendly send-times drop EU + AU + JP CTR 20–40% per Klaviyo per-locale 2024.

- In-region 3PL activated (EU + UK + AU at minimum)
- Per-market pricing-psychology research refreshed (PPP + round-number rule)
- JP APPI + per-market data-protection compliance audited
- **Test order flow verified end-to-end:** place test order from JP + DE + SE; verify everything from Gate B + per-market payment methods + JP Konbini flow

### Gate D — Steady-state international ops (Month 6+)

- International revenue ≥20% of total revenue (the gating threshold for Phase 3)
- International LTV:CAC ≥2.5:1 (lower than US 3:1 because of higher CAC in new markets; ≥2.5:1 is healthy)
- International contribution margin ≥20% (after duties + shipping + FX + 3PL costs)
- Per-market return-rate ≤8% (industry baseline 5–8% for apparel; 2–4% for non-apparel)
- IOSS/VAT MOSS quarterly filing up-to-date
- GDPR + UK GDPR + AU Privacy Act + JP APPI compliance audited annually
- Per-market CAC payback ≤9 months
- Per-market Triple Whale attribution view green (per-market revenue + per-market cohort-LTV)
- 12-month strategic-multiplier compounding: voice-localization CVR-lift + Klarna/AfterPay checkout-completion + Stage-3 markets layered on top
