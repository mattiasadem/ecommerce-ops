# Privacy & consent management — GDPR + UK-GDPR + CCPA/CPRA + PIPEDA + LGPD + cookie banner + CMP (Move #33)

> The privacy-engine that turns the operator's site from a "fine-on-arrival" liability into a "fine-resistant, consent-mode-v2-optimized, attribution-revenue-preserving" machine — Move #33 is the consent-management + data-subject-rights + cookie-banner + privacy-policy + CMP + consent-mode-v2 + data-broker-suppression + data-subject-request automation layer that protects against the canonical $50k-$20M GDPR-fine-and-revenue-tail-off trap, recovers EU/UK/CA/BR/AU revenue that goes dark without proper consent signals, and compounds the lift of Move #6 (Triple Whale attribution), Move #5 (Klaviyo + Postscript), Move #25 (International Expansion), Move #28 (Data Warehouse CDP), Move #36 (Email Deliverability), and Move #41 (Composable MACH) by ensuring the consent signals those tools depend on are captured, propagated, and acted on.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when ANY of the following apply:

- Brand sells (or plans to sell) to EU/UK/EEA customers — GDPR + UK-GDPR + ePrivacy apply; fines up to €20M / 4% of global revenue per violation per the ICO 2024 + CNIL 2024 + EDPB 2024 enforcement records.
- Brand sells (or plans to sell) to California / Virginia / Colorado / Connecticut / Utah / Texas / Florida / Oregon / Tennessee / Iowa / Montana / Indiana customers — CCPA + CPRA + 12+ state privacy laws apply; fines $2,500-$7,500 per violation + statutory damages $100-$750 per consumer per incident + AG / CPPA / private-right-of-action enforcement.
- Brand runs paid ads on Meta / Google / TikTok / Pinterest / Snap / Microsoft — Google's consent-mode-v2 (mandatory since March 2024 for EEA) + Meta's "Limited Data Use" flag + TikTok's EU-consent-flag + Pinterest's enhanced-consent all require explicit consent capture before the ad-platform can do modeled-conversion + audience-building; without it, attribution drops 30-60% AND the operator faces a 2%-4% global-revenue GDPR-fine risk.
- Brand runs email or SMS marketing — Klaviyo + Postscript + Attentive + SMSBump + Iterable + Customer.io all require explicit double-opt-in for EU/UK/CA/BR per GDPR Art-7 + CAN-SPAM + TCPA + LGPD; without consent capture, deliverability tanks (Move #36 Pitfall #5 — sending to non-consented recipients triggers spam-trap hits and ISP-throttling within 30 days).
- Brand ships Move #6 Triple Whale attribution OR Move #41 CDP — without consent-mode-v2, the post-iOS-14.5 attribution gap widens (Move #6 Triple-Whale-cohort-overlay reads "modeled conversions" which only fire with consent-mode-v2 captured; without it, cohort-LTV-overlay is blind).
- Brand has shipped Move #5 Klaviyo+Postscript OR Move #19 SMS orchestration OR Move #25 International expansion — each of those moves assumes a working consent layer underneath; without Move #33, those moves ship on a privacy-violation foundation that triggers a 30-day-to-fine timeline the moment a privacy regulator audits.
- Brand receives (or anticipates receiving) data-subject-access-requests (DSARs), right-to-be-forgotten requests, right-to-data-portability requests, right-to-opt-out-of-sale-or-sharing requests — GDPR Art-15 / Art-17 / Art-20 + CCPA 1798.105 / 1798.120 + 12+ state equivalents require response within 30-45 days; manual handling costs $50-$200/request and bottlenecks at 50+ requests/month.
- Brand uses Shopify, Recharge, Loop Returns, Smile.io, Yotpo, Klaviyo, Postscript, Gorgias, Triple Whale, Hotjar, FullStory, Microsoft Clarity, Meta Pixel, Google Ads Tag, TikTok Pixel, Pinterest Tag, Snap Pixel — every one of these scripts writes a non-essential cookie or transmits personal data to a 3rd-party processor; without a CMP, each one is a violation waiting for an audit.
- Brand wants to defend against class-action lawsuits ($1k-$50k statutory damages per consumer per CCPA + BIPA + state-equivalent) and AG investigations (CCPA: $2.5k-$7.5k per violation; CPRA: $100-$750 per consumer per incident OR $7.5k per intentional violation).
- Brand plans to ship Move #29 Composable/Headless commerce — composable stacks break the implicit consent layer that all-in-one platforms provide; without a CMP in front of the headless front-end, every page-load is a non-consented-data-fire.

Do NOT use this skill if:

- Brand has <100 orders/mo and 0 EU/UK/CA/BR/AU customers AND no plans to expand — defer to a single Cookiebot free or Iubenda free tier + a "Privacy Policy generated by Shopify defaults" page (the regulatory floor for a US-only <$100k-yr operation).
- Brand has already shipped Move #33 and is fully cookie-banner-CMP-compliant + DSAR-automated + consent-mode-v2-live — re-evaluate at the next major regulatory change (e.g., EU AI Act 2026 enforcement, ePrivacy Regulation finalization, US federal preemption).

## What "best in class" looks like

A best-in-class privacy & consent management stack for a $1M-$10M GMV DTC brand selling into US + EU + UK + CA + BR + AU looks like this:

**5-pillar canonical stack:**

1. **Cookie banner + Consent Management Platform (CMP)** — OneTrust Pro $375/mo Path B DEFAULT for $1M-$10M GMV OR Cookiebot Standard $12/mo for <$500k GMV OR Iubenda Pro $27/mo for $500k-$3M GMV OR Usercentrics Starter $0-$49/mo for <$1M GMV OR Ketch Business $600/mo for $5M+ GMV OR Osano Essential $0-$199/mo for $1M-$5M GMV OR Didomi Starter $250/mo OR TrustArc Essential $400/mo OR Termly Starter $100/mo. The CMP captures consent + writes a verifiable consent log + serves the cookie banner + manages granular opt-in/opt-out per category (necessary / preferences / statistics / marketing) + auto-geofences (EU/UK = opt-in / US-CA = opt-out / US-other = implied) + syncs consent signals to all downstream tags via the IAB TCF v2.2 framework.
2. **Consent-mode-v2 propagation to ad-platforms** — Google consent-mode-v2 (mandatory for EEA since March 2024; required for modeled-conversion in Chrome Privacy Sandbox) + Meta Limited Data Use flag + TikTok EU-consent-flag + Pinterest enhanced-consent + Snap enhanced-consent + Microsoft Clarity consent-state. Each ad-platform reads the consent signals from the CMP via gtag / fbq / ttq / pintrk / snaptr / clarity and either (a) withholds the event entirely if consent is denied OR (b) sends the cookieless pings that power modeled-conversion attribution per the ad-platform's privacy-safe modeling. Without this layer, the operator's ad-attribution drops 30-60% AND the operator faces regulatory exposure for non-consented data transfer.
3. **Klaviyo + Postscript + Gorgias + Triple Whale consent-aware propagation** — Klaviyo's built-in double-opt-in (mandatory for GDPR) + suppress-by-consent segmentation + per-profile consent-state + consent-timestamp + consent-evidence-receipt (the canonical "right-to-proof-of-consent" GDPR Art-7(1) requirement) + Postscript's per-recipient consent-state + Gorgias's "consent-on-ticket-create" + Triple Whale's consent-aware attribution (modeled-conversion only fires for consented users, raw conversion only for explicit consent).
4. **DSAR / Right-to-be-forgotten / Right-to-data-portability automation** — OneTrust DataSubjectRequest Automation (free w/ OneTrust) OR Ketch DSR Automation (free w/ Ketch) OR Transcend DSR ($300/mo) OR Datagrail DSR ($500/mo) OR Ethyca ($750/mo) OR Mine ($99/mo) + Shopify Customer Privacy API + Klaviyo profile-suppression + Postscript recipient-suppression + Gorgias ticket-redaction + Triple Whale identity-redaction + Hotjar session-redaction + FullStory session-redaction + Microsoft Clarity session-redaction. The automation orchestrates identity-resolution across the 15-30 3rd-party processors (Klaviyo + Postscript + Gorgias + Recharge + Loop + Smile + Yotpo + Triple Whale + Hotjar + FullStory + Clarity + Meta CAPI + Google EC + TikTok EAPI + Pinterest CAPI + Snap CAPI + Microsoft Ads + LinkedIn Ads + Segment + Snowplow + warehouse). Manual handling costs $50-$200/request and bottlenecks at 50+ requests/mo.
5. **Privacy policy + DPA registry + data-broker suppression + audit-trail** — Iubenda Privacy Policy generator (free w/ Iubenda) OR Termly Privacy Policy generator + OneTrust Data Mapping Automation + Datagrail Data Inventory + Mine Automated Data Broker Removal + DataBrokers Registry (CA + VT + OR per CCPA + state laws). The privacy policy covers 8+ jurisdictional templates (GDPR / UK-GDPR / CCPA / CPRA / PIPEDA / LGPD / Privacy Act / APPI); the DPA registry tracks every vendor's signed DPA + sub-processor list + breach-notification SLA + cross-border-transfer mechanism (SCCs / EU-US DPF / UK Extension / UK Addendum); the data-broker suppression ensures CA / VT / OR / TX residents can exercise "right-to-opt-out-of-sale-or-sharing" + "right-to-limit-use-of-sensitive-personal-information" against the 50+ data brokers that scrape ecommerce data.

**Default compliance surface** for a $3M US+EU DTC brand:

- **GDPR-compliant cookie banner** (IAB TCF v2.2 certified) — granular opt-in for EU/UK/EEA visitors, geo-fenced to the 27 EU + 3 EEA + UK jurisdictions; "Reject all" + "Accept all" + "Customize" buttons; consent-log-timestamped + verifiable-receipt.
- **CCPA/CPRA-compliant cookie banner** — "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link in footer + per-page "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link; per-CA-resident opt-out of sale/sharing + opt-out of cross-context-behavioral-advertising + limit-use-of-SPI.
- **PIPEDA / Quebec Law 25 compliant banner** — express-consent for CA residents; per-CA-resident PIPEDA-compliant data-handling; Law 25 privacy-impact-assessment for high-risk processing.
- **LGPD-compliant banner** — express-consent for BR residents; per-BR-resident LGPD-compliant data-handling; ANPD-registered DPO.
- **Privacy Act 1988 / APPI / PIPA / PDPA compliant** — express-consent for AU/JP/KR/SG residents; per-jurisdiction privacy-policy-template; cross-border-transfer-restrictions.
- **DSAR automation** — 30-day-SLA GDPR response + 45-day-SLA CCPA response + automatic identity-resolution + automatic suppression across 15-30 3rd-party processors; per-quarter DSAR-volume-report + per-DSAR-time-to-fulfill.
- **Consent-mode-v2 live on all ad-platforms** — Google consent-mode-v2 + Meta Limited Data Use + TikTok EU-consent-flag + Pinterest enhanced-consent + Snap enhanced-consent; attribution-degradation-monitoring + per-platform modeled-vs-raw-conversion-split.
- **Data-broker suppression** — auto-submit opt-out requests to the 50+ data brokers (Acxiom / Oracle / Experian / Equifax / TransUnion / Epsilon / LiveRamp / Salesforce DMP / Adobe Audience Manager / Oracle BlueKai) on a per-quarter cadence; verification that the broker has honored the opt-out within 30 days.
- **DPA + sub-processor registry** — signed DPA + sub-processor list + breach-notification SLA for each of the 50-100+ 3rd-party vendors; cross-border-transfer mechanism documented (SCCs / EU-US DPF / UK Extension / UK Addendum / BCRs).
- **Quarterly privacy audit** — per-quarter review of consent-mode-v2 propagation + DSAR-volume + cookie-banner-version + privacy-policy-version + DPA-renewal-status; per-quarter breach-notification-fire-drill (simulate a hypothetical breach, time-to-notification-SLA).
- **Annual privacy training** — per-year, all employees + all contractors + all 3rd-party-processors-with-PII-access complete GDPR/CCPA/PIPEDA/LGPD training; per-employee training-record-retained for 3 years per GDPR Art-39.

**Default success metrics (NOT just compliance checkbox):**

- **Consent rate** per region — % of EU/UK visitors who click "Accept all" or "Customize" vs "Reject all"; target ≥60% accept-all OR customized-accept; <40% consent-rate means the banner UX is broken and the operator's attribution + email-marketing revenue is throttled.
- **Consent-mode-v2 attribution-recovery** — % of conversions attributed via modeled-vs-raw; target ≥70% modeled for EEA users when consent is denied; <50% means the consent-mode-v2 wiring is broken and the operator is missing 30-60% of attribution-revenue.
- **DSAR volume + DSAR SLA** — DSARs/mo + DSAR-time-to-fulfill; target <30 days for GDPR + <45 days for CCPA; >30-day-fulfillment means a fine-risk.
- **Data-broker suppression rate** — % of opt-out requests honored within 30 days; target ≥90%; <70% means the operator's CA / VT / OR residents' right-to-opt-out is broken.
- **DPA coverage** — % of vendors with signed DPA + current sub-processor list; target 100%; <100% means the operator has unidentified sub-processor exposure.
- **Breach-notification SLA** — time-to-notify-from-incident-detection; target <72 hours per GDPR Art-33; >72 hours means a fine-risk.

## The build (time estimate)

**Default Path B for $1M-$10M GMV brand: 4-8 weeks part-time (8-12 hr/wk).**

### Phase 0 — Prereq gate (1 wk)

Before installing Move #33, ALL of the following must be true:

- [ ] Brand sells (or plans to sell) to EU/UK/EEA customers OR runs paid ads on Meta/Google/TikTok/Pinterest/Snap/Microsoft OR ships email/SMS marketing OR uses Shopify + Klaviyo + Postscript + Gorgias + Triple Whale + Hotjar/FullStory/Clarity (canonical "Move #33-required-for-Move-#5/#6/#25/#36/#41" rule per Klaviyo 2024 + Triple Whale 2024 + Google consent-mode-v2 2024 benchmarks).
- [ ] ≥$100k GMV/yr (canonical "Move #33-NOT-justified-at-<100k-GMV" floor per OneTrust 2024 + Cookiebot 2024 + Iubenda 2024 benchmarks — defer to Cookiebot Free or Iubenda Free at <$100k GMV).
- [ ] Move #6 Triple Whale attribution shipped OR scheduled in 30 days (canonical "consent-mode-v2-without-attribution = no-measurement-of-recovery" anti-pattern per Triple Whale 2024).
- [ ] Move #5 Klaviyo + Postscript shipped OR scheduled in 30 days (canonical "consent-without-ESP = consent-captured-but-not-acted-on" anti-pattern per Klaviyo 2024).
- [ ] ≥4 hr/wk operator commitment + 1 hr/wk legal-counsel commitment (for DPA review + cross-border-transfer-mechanism documentation + privacy-policy-template-jurisdiction-selection).

### Phase 1 — Audit + CMP selection (Days 1-3)

- Day 1: Run a privacy-audit. List every 3rd-party script + cookie + pixel + tag on the site (use OneTrust's free scan OR Cookiebot's free scan OR a manual audit). Document per-script: name / vendor / cookie-name / purpose / consent-category / cross-border-transfer / DPA-status / sub-processor-list. This becomes the data-mapping registry.
- Day 2: Select the CMP. For <$500k GMV → Cookiebot Standard $12/mo. For $500k-$3M GMV → Iubenda Pro $27/mo OR Osano Essential $99/mo. For $1M-$10M GMV → OneTrust Pro $375/mo Path B DEFAULT OR Usercentrics Starter $0-$49/mo. For $5M+ GMV → Ketch Business $600/mo OR Didomi Starter $250/mo OR TrustArc Essential $400/mo. Verify the CMP is IAB TCF v2.2 certified (canonical "non-IAB-TCF-CMP = EU-regulator-rejection" anti-pattern per EDPB 2024 + IAB Europe 2024).
- Day 3: Document the DPA + cross-border-transfer-mechanism for each vendor. Per-vendor: signed-DPA-status / sub-processor-list / breach-notification-SLA / cross-border-transfer-mechanism (SCCs / EU-US DPF / UK Extension / UK Addendum / BCRs). For vendors without a signed DPA → request one before the next purchase-order-renewal.

### Phase 2 — Banner deploy + consent capture (Days 4-7)

- Day 4: Deploy the cookie banner on the site. Geo-fence EU/UK/EEA visitors to opt-in (Reject all / Accept all / Customize) + geo-fence US-CA visitors to opt-out (Do Not Sell or Share) + geo-fence US-other visitors to implied (banner shown for transparency only). Use the CMP's IAB TCF v2.2 template + granular consent-categories (necessary / preferences / statistics / marketing).
- Day 5: Wire the CMP to the 15-30 3rd-party scripts on the site. Each script reads the consent-state from the CMP via the CMP's JS-SDK; non-essential scripts (analytics / marketing / preferences) are blocked until consent is granted. The CMP writes a verifiable consent-log (timestamp + consent-state + consent-evidence-receipt + per-category-acceptance) per GDPR Art-7(1) + ePrivacy + CCPA + CPRA + LGPD.
- Day 6: Deploy consent-mode-v2 on Google Ads + Limited Data Use on Meta Ads + EU-consent-flag on TikTok Ads + enhanced-consent on Pinterest Ads + Snap enhanced-consent + Microsoft Clarity consent-state. Each ad-platform's pixel/SDK reads the consent-state from the CMP and either (a) withholds the event entirely if consent is denied OR (b) sends the cookieless pings that power modeled-conversion. Without this step, the operator's ad-attribution drops 30-60% AND the operator faces regulatory exposure.
- Day 7: Verify the consent-capture is propagated to Klaviyo + Postscript + Gorgias + Triple Whale. Klaviyo's per-profile consent-state must show "consent captured at <timestamp> via <CMP-name>" + Postscript's per-recipient consent-state must show the same + Triple Whale's consent-aware-attribution must show modeled-conversion events for consented users + raw-conversion events for explicit-consent users.

### Phase 3 — DSAR automation + data-broker suppression (Days 8-14)

- Days 8-10: Deploy DSAR automation. Wire the CMP's DSAR-portal OR a third-party DSAR-automation tool (Transcend / Datagrail / Ethyca / Mine / OneTrust DataSubjectRequest / Ketch DSR) to: (a) receive the DSAR via web-form OR email; (b) verify identity (per CCPA / GDPR); (c) orchestrates identity-resolution across Klaviyo + Postscript + Gorgias + Recharge + Loop + Smile + Yotpo + Triple Whale + Hotjar + FullStory + Clarity + Shopify; (d) pulls all PII for the requester; (e) applies the action (provide / delete / opt-out / portability / limit-use-of-SPI); (f) logs the action with timestamp + evidence-receipt. SLA: <30 days for GDPR, <45 days for CCPA.
- Days 11-12: Deploy data-broker-suppression. Wire the CMP's data-broker-suppression module OR Mine OR a manual quarterly cadence to submit opt-out requests to the 50+ data brokers (Acxiom / Oracle / Experian / Equifax / TransUnion / Epsilon / LiveRamp / Salesforce DMP / Adobe Audience Manager / Oracle BlueKai). Verify the broker has honored the opt-out within 30 days.
- Days 13-14: Document the breach-notification SOP. Per GDPR Art-33: 72-hour-SLA from incident-detection to supervisory-authority-notification. Per CCPA: per-CA-AG notification. Per state-equivalent laws: per-state-AG notification. Per LGPD: ANPD notification. Document: who-does-what-when / which-vendors-must-be-notified / which-authorities-must-be-notified / which-data-subjects-must-be-notified / what-evidence-must-be-retained.

### Phase 4 — Privacy-policy + DPA registry + audit (Days 15-21)

- Days 15-17: Generate the privacy-policy via Iubenda OR Termly OR OneTrust. Cover the 8+ jurisdictional templates that apply to the operator's customer base (GDPR / UK-GDPR / CCPA / CPRA / PIPEDA / Law 25 / LGPD / Privacy Act / APPI / PIPA / PDPA / PCPO). Include: data-collected / purpose-of-processing / legal-basis-for-processing / data-retention-period / third-party-recipients / cross-border-transfer-mechanism / data-subject-rights / contact-information-for-DPO-or-representative / automated-decision-making-or-profiling / changes-to-policy + effective-date.
- Days 18-19: Build the DPA + sub-processor registry. Per-vendor: signed-DPA-status / sub-processor-list / breach-notification-SLA / cross-border-transfer-mechanism / data-retention-period / purpose-of-processing. - Days 20-21: Run the first quarterly privacy-audit. Per-vendor: consent-capture-propagation-correct / DSAR-automation-tested / data-broker-suppression-tested / privacy-policy-current / DPA-current / cross-border-transfer-mechanism-documented / breach-notification-SOP-current. Document any gaps + remediation-timeline. Schedule the next quarterly audit on the operator's calendar.

## Common pitfalls (18 from real builds)

1. **Geo-fence logic wrong (EU visitors served implied-consent banner instead of opt-in banner)** — GDPR Art-7 requires explicit opt-in BEFORE any non-essential cookie fires; an implied-consent banner for EU visitors is the canonical "you'll get fined €20M in your first year" anti-pattern. **Fix:** CMP's geo-IP-detection must map every EU/UK/EEA IP to the opt-in template + every US-CA IP to the opt-out template + every other IP to the implied template; test with a UK VPN + a French VPN + a German VPN + a California VPN before going live.

2. **"Reject all" button doesn't actually reject (canonical dark-pattern anti-pattern)** — EDPB 2024 cookie-banner-study found 65% of banners have a "Reject all" button that is visually subordinate to "Accept all" or requires more clicks than "Accept all" or sets non-essential cookies even when "Reject all" is clicked. **Fix:** the "Reject all" button must be visually equivalent to "Accept all" (same size + same color + same position) + must immediately set all non-essential cookies to denied + must log the rejection-event with verifiable consent-evidence-receipt per GDPR Art-7(1).

3. **Pre-ticked checkboxes (canonical GDPR Art-7 violation)** — EDPB guidelines on consent (2024) explicitly state pre-ticked checkboxes are NOT valid consent; the user must perform an affirmative action to opt-in. **Fix:** every non-essential cookie must default to OFF + the user must click "Accept" or "Customize → check the category" to opt-in; verify per-page that no pre-ticked checkboxes are rendered.

4. **Cookie banner not on every page (canonical "consent-capture-on-homepage-only" anti-pattern)** — GDPR + ePrivacy require consent-capture on EVERY page that sets a non-essential cookie; a banner on the homepage only is the canonical "you'll get fined when the DPA audits a product-detail-page-with-3-pixels" anti-pattern. **Fix:** the banner must be a site-wide overlay that fires on first-page-load AND persists across page-navigations; verify per-page that the banner is present on 100% of pages (not just the homepage).

5. **Consent-mode-v2 not wired to Google Ads (canonical "March-2024-EEA-modeled-conversion-blackout" anti-pattern)** — Google made consent-mode-v2 mandatory for EEA in March 2024; without it, modeled-conversion is disabled and the operator's Google-Ads attribution drops 50-70% in EEA. **Fix:** install Google consent-mode-v2 via the CMP's JS-SDK + verify the gtag('consent', 'update', {...}) call fires on every page-load + verify the 4 consent-states (ad_storage / analytics_storage / ad_user_data / ad_personalization) are propagated correctly.

6. **Meta Limited Data Use flag not set (canonical "Meta-Audience-Network-banned" anti-pattern)** — Meta's "Limited Data Use" flag must be set when a CA-resident user opts out of sale/sharing; without it, Meta treats the user as opted-in and includes them in audience-building that violates CCPA. **Fix:** wire the CMP's CA-resident-detection → Meta Pixel's "ldu" field via fbq('dataProcessingOptions', ['LDU'], 0, 0); verify per-CA-resident that the LDU flag is set.

7. **Klaviyo double-opt-in not enforced for EU/UK (canonical Klaviyo-GDPR-bypass)** — GDPR Art-7(1) requires explicit opt-in; Klaviyo's default single-opt-in is the canonical "you'll get GDPR-fined when the ICO audits your EU subscriber list" anti-pattern. **Fix:** enable Klaviyo's per-list double-opt-in for ALL EU/UK/EEA subscribers + verify the welcome-email + consent-confirmation-email fires + verify the consent-timestamp is captured on the Klaviyo profile + verify the consent-evidence-receipt is stored.

8. **Postscript consent-capture not wired (canonical "TCPA-fine-for-non-consented-SMS" anti-pattern)** — TCPA + 10+ state mini-TCPA laws require express-written-consent for marketing-SMS; Postscript's default opt-in is the canonical "you'll get fined $500-$1,500 per non-consented-text" anti-pattern. **Fix:** enable Postscript's per-recipient double-opt-in for ALL US/CA/EU subscribers + verify the consent-timestamp is captured + verify the consent-method (form-checkbox / keyword-opt-in / paper-signup) is documented.

9. **DSAR response exceeds 30-day-SLA (canonical "GDPR-fine-for-late-DSAR" anti-pattern)** — GDPR Art-12(3) requires DSAR response within 30 days (extendable to 60 days for complex requests); CCPA requires 45 days. Manual handling bottlenecks at 50+ requests/mo. **Fix:** deploy DSAR-automation (OneTrust DSR / Ketch DSR / Transcend / Datagrail / Ethyca / Mine) that orchestrates identity-resolution across the 15-30 3rd-party processors; target <30-day-fulfillment for 95%+ of DSARs.

10. **Data-broker opt-out requests not honored (canonical "right-to-opt-out-broken" anti-pattern)** — CCPA / VT / OR / TX require opt-out-from-sale-or-sharing to be honored within 15-30 days; data brokers notoriously ignore opt-outs for 30-90 days. **Fix:** submit opt-out to each of the 50+ brokers + verify the opt-out has been honored within 30 days (use Privacy Rights Clearinghouse OR a manual quarterly check); re-submit for any broker that hasn't honored.

11. **Privacy-policy-template not jurisdiction-correct (canonical "policy-says-California-when-it-should-say-GDPR" anti-pattern)** — a single-template privacy-policy that doesn't cover the operator's actual jurisdictions is a fine-on-arrival risk. **Fix:** use Iubenda OR Termly OR OneTrust to generate per-jurisdiction templates (GDPR / UK-GDPR / CCPA / CPRA / PIPEDA / Law 25 / LGPD / Privacy Act / APPI / PIPA / PDPA / PCPO) + verify the policy covers data-collected / purpose-of-processing / legal-basis / data-retention / third-party-recipients / cross-border-transfer-mechanism / data-subject-rights / DPO-contact.

12. **DPA + sub-processor-list not current (canonical "vendor-rotates-sub-processors-without-notification" anti-pattern)** — GDPR Art-28(2) requires the processor to notify the controller of sub-processor changes; many vendors update sub-processors quarterly without notification. **Fix:** subscribe to each vendor's sub-processor-change-notification + per-quarter review the sub-processor-list + update the DPA-registry + notify the operator's customers if the sub-processor-list change is material.

13. **Cross-border-transfer-mechanism missing or expired (canonical "Schrems-II-non-compliance" anti-pattern)** — EU-US Privacy Shield invalidated by Schrems-II (2020); EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF) effective 2023 but requires self-certification; UK Extension + UK Addendum required for UK transfers. **Fix:** verify each vendor's cross-border-transfer-mechanism is current (SCCs 2021/914 + UK Addendum + EU-US DPF self-certification per applicable jurisdiction); document the mechanism in the DPA-registry.

14. **Breach-notification SOP exists but not tabletop-tested (canonical "72-hour-clock-starts-without-knowing-it" anti-pattern)** — GDPR Art-33 requires 72-hour breach-notification from incident-detection to supervisory-authority-notification; many operators discover they don't know who their DPO is / who their supervisory authority is / what their incident-detection mechanism is until a breach happens. **Fix:** run a quarterly tabletop exercise — simulate a hypothetical breach, time the clock from detection to notification, document gaps, remediate.

15. **Cookie banner blocking site rendering (canonical "consent-rate-collapses-when-banner-blocks-ATC" anti-pattern)** — a heavy / slow cookie banner that blocks the page-render or the ATC-button for 1-3 seconds is the canonical "60%-of-visitors-bounce-before-consent" anti-pattern. **Fix:** use a lightweight CMP (Cookiebot / Iubenda / Usercentrics) + ensure the banner renders in <500ms + ensure the "Reject all" button is immediately clickable + ensure the page is fully interactive behind the banner (don't block scroll / don't block ATC).

16. **Consented-users-mistakenly-segmented-as-non-consented (canonical "Klaviyo-suppression-of-consented-subscribers" anti-pattern)** — a CMP-to-Klaviyo sync that suppresses consented subscribers because the consent-timestamp is missing or the consent-method doesn't match Klaviyo's expected format is the canonical "Move #5 revenue-tank-from-consent-sync-bug" anti-pattern. **Fix:** verify the CMP-to-Klaviyo sync is bidirectional (consent-granted in CMP → Klaviyo profile updates to "subscribed"; consent-revoked in CMP → Klaviyo profile updates to "suppressed"); verify the consent-timestamp + consent-method + consent-evidence-receipt are all captured on the Klaviyo profile.

17. **Per-quarter privacy-audit not on calendar (canonical "Move-#33-degrades-without-cadence" anti-pattern)** — regulatory landscape changes 1-2x/yr (new state law, new EU guidance, new ad-platform consent-mode update); without a quarterly-audit cadence, the operator's compliance drifts. **Fix:** schedule a 1-hr-per-quarter privacy-audit on the operator's calendar + run the 12-gate verification suite + document gaps + remediation-timeline.

18. **Operator confuses "consent-mode-v2" with "consent-capture" (canonical "wiring-confusion" anti-pattern)** — consent-capture = the CMP captures the consent + writes the consent-log; consent-mode-v2 = the ad-platform receives the consent-state + either fires-or-withholds the event. They're TWO separate steps. Many operators deploy consent-capture but skip consent-mode-v2 wiring, OR deploy consent-mode-v2 but skip consent-capture. **Fix:** verify BOTH steps are deployed + verify the consent-state is propagated from CMP → ad-platform-pixel/SDK + verify the per-platform attestation (Google Ads consent-mode-v2 dashboard / Meta LDU dashboard / TikTok EU-consent-flag dashboard / Pinterest enhanced-consent dashboard).
