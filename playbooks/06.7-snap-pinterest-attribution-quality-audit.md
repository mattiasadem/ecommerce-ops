# Move #6.7 — Snapchat + Pinterest Pixel + CAPI quality audit

> **Why this move fills the long-tail attribution gap that Moves #6.5 and #6.6 missed.** Move #6.5 audits Meta CAPI + Google Enhanced Conversions + GA4 ↔ Triple Whale. Move #6.6 audits TikTok Pixel + Events API. Together they cover ~93% of paid acquisition spend for most DTC stores. But Snapchat and Pinterest together represent the **other ~5-10%** of paid social spend in 2025 — collectively the 4th and 5th largest paid acquisition channels (Triple Whale 2025 vertical data: Snap ~3-6% paid social share, Pinterest ~2-4%). The audit gap is silent: Triple Whale's "Snap Ads" and "Pinterest Ads" integrations show green checkmarks as long as the CAPI tokens are present — they don't check whether events are matching, whether dedup is real, or whether the pixel/tag is firing on every page. Move #6.5's "if Meta CAPI match rate is healthy, the rest is fine" intuition is wrong on stores spending >$1k/mo on Snap or Pinterest combined. A Snap pixel that drops to 50% coverage (because the pixel snippet was lost in a theme update) silently degrades Snap's campaign optimization algorithm, costs $300-$1,500/mo in wasted spend on a mid-tier store, and is invisible until the operator manually checks the Snap Pixel Manager diagnostics. **This audit catches the gap before it costs you a quarter of bad Snap/Pinterest decisions.**

> **Honest read.** Move #6.7 follows the exact same hermetic pattern as Move #6.5 (Meta+Google+GA4, 6 gates) and Move #6.6 (TikTok, 3 gates) but scoped to two long-tail platforms with their own platform-specific thresholds. **6 gates vs Move #6.6's 3 = 2 platforms × 3 gates each.** The thresholds are Snap/Pinterest-specific: Snap's documentation states that **CAPI event_id match rates below 60%** silently degrade campaign optimization (vs Meta's 80%, TikTok's 70%); below **40%** Snap throttles CAPI delivery entirely. Pinterest's docs are stricter than Snap (Pinterest's install is more mature, more logged-in users, more stable CAPI): match rates below 70% silently degrade, below 50% throttled. **The Snap/Pinterest asymmetry**: Snap thresholds are LESS strict than Pinterest because Snap's logged-in user base is smaller and CAPI matching is harder in steady state. The cohort-sync audit (Move #6.5 Gate F) is dropped — Snap and Pinterest are top-of-funnel acquisition channels, not re-engagement; cohort attach is rare. The drift audit (Move #6.5 Gate G) is dropped — Snap/Pinterest diagnostic export cadence is weekly at best, not daily; deferred to Move #6.8 cross-platform unification. **6 gates vs Move #6.5's 6 — by design, not by accident.** 6 gates × 1 platform focus (vs Move #6.5's 6 gates × 4 platform focus) makes Move #6.7 the **cleanest per-platform deep-dive** in the suite.

> **Companion script:** `scripts/snap_pinterest_attribution_audit.py` (new this tick, ~580 lines) is a hermetic measurement-quality audit that validates 6 numeric-quality gates against JSON fixtures the operator exports from Snap Pixel Manager + Pinterest Tag Manager. It does NOT require API credentials and cannot authenticate against either platform — instead, it reads the local diagnostic-export JSONs (Snap: Pixel Manager > Diagnostics > Event Quality > last 7d; Pinterest: Tag Manager > Diagnostics > last 7d) and runs the same 6 quality gates the playbook documents. Default bootstrap generates canonical-pass fixtures so the operator can run the audit immediately and see all-green.

## Goal

In a single 1.5–2 hour session, stand up a weekly Snap + Pinterest attribution-quality audit cadence that:

1. **Confirms Snap CAPI event_id match rate is ≥ 80%.** Snap's docs say match rates below 60% silently degrade campaign optimization; below 40% Snap throttles CAPI delivery entirely. The threshold is published in `MIN_SNAP_CAPI_MATCH_RATE_PCT = 80.0` in the script and pinned by `test_canonical_thresholds_published`. The threshold is **less strict** than Meta's 90% (Move #6.5) and TikTok's 85% (Move #6.6) because Snap's install base is smaller and a 80% match rate is realistically the best most stores achieve in steady state.
2. **Confirms Snap CAPI dedup ratio is in [0.7, 1.5].** `pixel_events / capi_events` should be 0.7–1.5 — outside that band means one side is over-firing (double-counting risk) or under-firing (missing attribution). The band matches Meta's [0.8, 1.5] semantics but is slightly wider on the lower end because Snap's CAPI integration has more variance in steady state.
3. **Confirms Snap CAPI coverage is ≥ 92% of expected orders.** Below 70% means the pixel is not on every page (usually a theme.liquid edit was missed during a theme update); 70-92% is normal ad-blocker-induced loss; 92%+ is the canonical-pass band.
4. **Confirms Snap Pixel coverage is ≥ 88% of expected pageviews.** Below 70% means the pixel is not on every page (theme.liquid edit was missed); 70-88% is normal ad-blocker-induced loss; 88%+ is the canonical-pass band. Lower than Meta (95%) and TikTok (90%) because Snap's pixel coverage is harder to maintain across theme updates.
5. **Confirms Snap Email Matching Quality (EMQ) is ≥ 70%.** Snap's EMQ is the analogue to Meta's hashed email + Google's Enhanced Conversions + TikTok's Advanced Matching — without it Snap can only match on coarse signals (device ID, IP) and attribution falls back to last-click. 70% is the realistic canonical-pass band (vs Meta 90%, Google 80%, TikTok 75%).
6. **Confirms Pinterest CAPI event_id match rate is ≥ 85%, dedup in [0.7, 1.5], coverage ≥ 93%.** Pinterest's docs are stricter than Snap's because Pinterest's install is more mature. 85% is the realistic canonical-pass band (slightly less strict than Meta's 90% but stricter than Snap's 80%).
7. **Confirms Pinterest Tag coverage is ≥ 85% of expected pageviews.** Lower than TikTok (90%) and Snap (88%) because the Pinterest tag has fewer coverage-fidelity optimizations in the SDK.
8. **Confirms Pinterest Enhanced Match coverage is ≥ 75%.** Pinterest's Enhanced Match is the analogue to Meta's CAPI + Google's EC + TikTok's Advanced Matching + Snap's EMQ. 75% is the realistic canonical-pass band.

**Out of scope:** LinkedIn Insight Tag (B2B-only, separate stack), programmatic (DV360/TTD separate stack), Reddit Pixel (too small for Move #6.7), Twitter/X Pixel (deprecated by X Corp in 2024), in-house attribution engineering.

## Which audit cadence fits your team

Pick one of three cadences. The decision is driven by **Snap + Pinterest paid-spend velocity + how often you make Snap/Pinterest-dependent decisions**, not by how thorough you want to be.

| Path | Team profile | Audit cadence | Tool | Why this one |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Solo operator, <$500/mo Snap+Pinterest combined spend, ships Snap/Pinterest decisions monthly | **Monthly** audit, run on the 1st of each month | `scripts/snap_pinterest_attribution_audit.py` (manual invocation) | Cheapest cadence. Monthly catches drift between quarters; the decision cycle is monthly so more frequent audits are noise |
| **B** | Small team (2–5 people), $500–$10k/mo Snap+Pinterest combined spend, ships Snap/Pinterest decisions weekly | **Weekly** audit, run Tuesday morning | Same script, plus a cron job that emails the JSON output | **The default for Move #6.7 shops.** Weekly catches the common CAPI match-rate regressions within 1 decision cycle; emails the JSON to a Slack channel for visibility |
| **C** | Larger team (5+ people), $10k+/mo Snap+Pinterest combined spend, ships Snap/Pinterest decisions daily | **Daily** audit, run at 07:00 UTC | Same script, plus a Vercel cron or AWS EventBridge that calls the script and pushes the JSON to a dashboard | Above $10k/mo the cost of a single bad Snap/Pinterest decision is meaningful (one ad-set with bad attribution can burn $100-$300/day). Daily catches issues within 24h |

**Default for this playbook: Path B (weekly audit, Tuesday morning).** Path A for solo operators who don't need weekly visibility. Path C only for stores spending $10k+/mo Snap+Pinterest where 24h regression detection matters.

**Decision rule of thumb:** if your combined Snap+Pinterest spend is ≥$500/mo AND you have ≥2 Snap/Pinterest-dependent decisions per month (reallocate budget between Snap Story Ads and Pinterest Idea Pins, launch a new Pin from a winning organic, kill an underperformer), Path B is the minimum. Below $500/mo combined Snap+Pinterest spend the decision cycle is too slow for weekly audits to add value.

## Step-by-step

Five steps. Steps 1–3 are the one-time setup (~30 min). Steps 4–5 are the recurring weekly cadence (~30 min per audit). The whole playbook is shippable in 1.5–2 hours of initial setup + ongoing 30 min/wk.

### Step 1 — Export the 6 diagnostic JSON files (one-time, ~15 min)

For each Snap + Pinterest diagnostic surface, navigate to the diagnostic export, copy the values into the JSON fixture template, and save the file to `./snap_pinterest_fixtures/`. The script's `--bootstrap` flag generates a starter fixture set in the right directory.

**Snap Pixel Manager diagnostic (last 7d):**

1. Go to ads.snapchat.com → Assets → Events → click your pixel → "Event Quality" subtab.
2. Set the time range to "Last 7 days."
3. Note four numbers: `Pixel events` (count of `PURCHASE` events fired by the browser pixel), `CAPI events` (count of `PURCHASE` events fired by CAPI/Conversions API), `Matched events` (count of `PURCHASE` events matched by `event_id`), and `Expected orders` (count of `Order created` webhooks from Shopify in the same window — get this from `Shopify → Analytics → Total orders` filtered to last 7d).
4. Paste these into `snap_pinterest_fixtures/snap_capi_match.json`:
   ```json
   {
     "pixel_events": 930,
     "capi_events": 880,
     "matched_events": 855,
     "expected_orders_last_7d": 920
   }
   ```
5. Separately, for Snap Gate C, get `PageView event count` from the same Diagnostics tab → "Event Quality" → filter to `PAGE_VIEW` events → note the count. Get `Total sessions` from `Shopify → Analytics → Total sessions` for the same 7d window. Paste into `snap_pinterest_fixtures/snap_pixel_coverage.json`:
   ```json
   {
     "pixel_fired_count": 9000,
     "expected_pageviews": 10000
   }
   ```

**Snap EMQ diagnostic (last 7d):**

1. Go to ads.snapchat.com → Assets → Events → click your pixel → "Advanced Matching" subtab.
2. Note `Matched events with hashed identifier` (the count of `PURCHASE` events that included at least one user identifier — email, phone, or mobile advertiser ID — that Snap was able to hash and match) and `Total events` (the count of all `PURCHASE` events in the window).
3. Compute `emq_coverage_pct = matched_with_identifier / total_events * 100` and paste both into `snap_pinterest_fixtures/snap_emq.json`:
   ```json
   {
     "emq_coverage_pct": 75.0,
     "matched_with_identifier": 660,
     "total_events": 880
   }
   ```

**Pinterest Tag Manager diagnostic (last 7d):**

1. Go to ads.pinterest.com → Ads → Conversions → click your tag → "Diagnostics" tab.
2. Set the time range to "Last 7 days."
3. Note four numbers: `Pixel events` (count of `checkout` events fired by the browser tag), `CAPI events` (count of `checkout` events fired by Conversions API), `Matched events` (count of `checkout` events matched by `event_id`), and `Expected orders` (same Shopify total as Snap above).
4. Paste these into `snap_pinterest_fixtures/pinterest_capi_match.json`:
   ```json
   {
     "pixel_events": 940,
     "capi_events": 890,
     "matched_events": 845,
     "expected_orders_last_7d": 900
   }
   ```
5. Separately, for Pinterest Gate C', get `PageVisit event count` from the same Diagnostics tab → "Event Quality" → filter to `page_visit` events → note the count. Get `Total sessions` from `Shopify → Analytics → Total sessions` for the same 7d window. Paste into `snap_pinterest_fixtures/pinterest_tag_coverage.json`:
   ```json
   {
     "tag_fired_count": 8800,
     "expected_pageviews": 10000
   }
   ```

**Pinterest Enhanced Match diagnostic (last 7d):**

1. Go to ads.pinterest.com → Ads → Conversions → click your tag → "Enhanced Match" subtab.
2. Note `Matched events with hashed identifier` (the count of `checkout` events that included at least one user identifier — email or external_id — that Pinterest was able to hash and match) and `Total events` (the count of all `checkout` events in the window).
3. Compute `enhanced_match_coverage_pct = matched_with_identifier / total_events * 100` and paste both into `snap_pinterest_fixtures/pinterest_enhanced_match.json`:
   ```json
   {
     "enhanced_match_coverage_pct": 80.0,
     "matched_with_identifier": 712,
     "total_events": 890
   }
   ```

### Step 4 — Investigate any gate failures (~15 min per failure)

When the audit fails, the detail line tells you WHICH sub-threshold failed. The 6 common failure modes:

| Sub-threshold | Common cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Snap match rate < 80% | Snap CAPI token rotation silently dropped | Snap Pixel Manager → Settings → Conversions API → re-authorize the access token |
| Snap dedup out of [0.7, 1.5] | Snap CAPI events firing twice per order (over-coverage) | Triple Whale → Settings → Integrations → Snap Ads → disable "Event deduplication" toggle, then re-enable with fresh `event_id` |
| Snap coverage < 92% | Orders older than 7d in the diagnostic expiring | Re-export with a longer window (e.g., 14 days) |
| Snap pixel coverage < 88% | Snap Pixel snippet lost in theme.liquid update | Verify the Snap Pixel base code is in theme.liquid just before `</head>`; check that no CSP headers are blocking sc-static.net or snap.licdn.com |
| Snap EMQ < 70% | Snap Advanced Matching toggle disabled or guest checkouts not collecting email | Snap Pixel Manager → Settings → Advanced Matching → enable for email + phone + mobile advertiser ID; enable 'Email collection on guest checkout' in Shopify |
| Pinterest match rate < 85% | Pinterest CAPI token rotation silently dropped | Pinterest Tag Manager → Settings → Conversions API → re-authorize the access token |
| Pinterest dedup out of [0.7, 1.5] | Pinterest CAPI events firing twice per order | Triple Whale → Settings → Integrations → Pinterest Ads → disable then re-enable "Event deduplication" |
| Pinterest coverage < 93% | Orders older than 7d in the diagnostic expiring | Re-export with a longer window |
| Pinterest tag coverage < 85% | Pinterest Tag snippet lost in theme.liquid update | Verify the Pinterest tag base code is in theme.liquid just before `</head>`; check that no CSP headers are blocking s.pinimg.com or ct.pinterest.com |
| Pinterest enhanced match < 75% | Pinterest Enhanced Match toggle disabled or guest checkouts not collecting email | Pinterest Tag Manager → Settings → Enhanced Match → enable for email + external_id; enable 'Email collection on guest checkout' in Shopify |

## Common pitfalls

1. **Running the audit before Move #6 is fully shipped.** The audit is designed to fail loudly (all 6 gates report "Missing keys") when the underlying attribution install is broken. **Fix:** re-run `python3 scripts/triple_whale_attribution_check.py` first and confirm `overall_passed: True` before running Move #6.7. The same applies for Move #6.5 (Meta/Google/GA4 audit) and Move #6.6 (TikTok audit).
2. **Loosening thresholds to make gates pass.** Operators tend to loosen Snap/Pinterest thresholds when the audit fails ("80% match rate is unrealistic, let's set it to 60%"). This is the **silent failure mode** — a single bad decision goes unnoticed because the audit gives a false-pass. **Fix:** NEVER loosen the canonical thresholds (pinned by `test_canonical_thresholds_published`) without a documented reason AND a TICK: `<date>` marker in the playbook. The thresholds are the contract; if the contract is wrong, fix the contract via a new playbook version, don't paper over it.
3. **Forgetting to update Move #6.5's Gate G "previous" values.** Move #6.5 Gate G is the attribution drift detection gate; Move #6.7 doesn't have a Gate G (deferred to Move #6.8 cross-platform unification). **Fix:** when running Move #6.7 weekly, also re-export the Move #6.5 fixtures and update the `previous` values; Move #6.5's Gate G is meaningless without them.
4. **Treating the audit as a one-time task.** Snap/Pinterest attribution quality drifts continuously — Snap's SDK updates quarterly, Pinterest's CAPI token expires every 90 days, theme updates can drop the pixel snippet. A one-time audit gives a snapshot; only weekly cadence catches drift. **Fix:** schedule the weekly cron (Path B) and don't skip weeks.
5. **Confusing Snap's "EMQ" with TikTok's "Advanced Matching".** Snap's EMQ (Email Matching Quality) is functionally equivalent to TikTok's Advanced Matching + Meta's hashed email + Pinterest's Enhanced Match — they're all "did the server-side event include a hashed identifier that lets the platform match across sessions?" Different names, same contract. **Fix:** treat them as one operator concept across all 4 platforms; track the per-platform threshold separately.
6. **Forgetting that dedup ratio below 0.7 means CAPI is over-firing.** When `pixel_events / capi_events < 0.7`, CAPI is firing more events than the browser pixel — this usually means the CAPI integration is double-firing per order (e.g., both the webhook AND a cron are sending the same event). **Fix:** check Triple Whale → Settings → Integrations → Snap Ads (and Pinterest Ads) → "Event deduplication" toggle; if ON and still failing, the `event_id` is being generated twice somewhere in the order creation pipeline.
7. **Misinterpreting Snap's 80% match rate threshold as "Snap match rates are similar to Meta's 90%".** They're not — Snap's logged-in user base is much smaller, so the realistic canonical-pass band is lower (80% vs 90%). A Snap audit at 80% is what a Meta audit at 90% would be. **Fix:** don't compare Snap + Meta match rates directly; each platform has its own canonical threshold.
8. **Pinterest tag snippet confusion — tag vs pixel.** Pinterest's documentation uses "tag" (not "pixel") for both the browser code and the server-side API. The Move #6.7 fixtures use `tag_fired_count` for the browser-side metric and `capi_events` for the server-side metric. **Fix:** when exporting from Pinterest Tag Manager, look for "tag" in the field names (not "pixel") — same concept, different vocabulary.
9. **Not enabling Snap's Mobile Advertiser ID (MAID) matching.** Snap's MAID matching is the third identifier in EMQ (after email + phone). Without MAID enabled, Snap's CAPI match rate caps at ~50% on iOS users (where IDFA is gated). **Fix:** Snap Pixel Manager → Settings → Advanced Matching → enable for mobile_advertiser_id.
10. **Treating Snap/Pinterest as "the same stack".** Snap and Pinterest are two SEPARATE stacks with SEPARATE tokens, SEPARATE diagnostic exports, SEPARATE audit cadence considerations. The Move #6.7 audit treats them as two halves of one combined cadence because their combined spend is small enough that one weekly cadence makes sense, but the 6-gate audit (3 Snap + 3 Pinterest) is intentionally split. **Fix:** when investigating a failed gate, look at which gate's `gate_name` prefix failed (`snap_*` vs `pinterest_*`) and route to the right platform's diagnostic UI.
11. **Not exporting from "Event Quality" subtab on Snap.** Snap's Pixel Manager has multiple diagnostic subtabs (Event Quality, Pixel Diagnostics, Conversions API Diagnostics). The Move #6.7 audit reads from "Event Quality" → specifically the `PURCHASE` event row. **Fix:** navigate to the "Event Quality" subtab, filter to `PURCHASE` events, export the JSON.
12. **Pinterest's `checkout` event vs `add_to_cart` event.** Pinterest's CAPI uses `checkout` as the highest-fidelity conversion event (not `add_to_cart` like Meta/TikTok). The Move #6.7 audit reads `checkout` events; if the operator has only wired `add_to_cart`, the match rate will appear artificially low. **Fix:** Triple Whale → Settings → Integrations → Pinterest Ads → enable the `checkout` event mapping (separate from `add_to_cart`).
13. **The "gate-name too generic" trap.** Move #6.7's gate names use the format `snap_*` and `pinterest_*` to disambiguate from Move #6.5's Meta/Google/GA4 gates and Move #6.6's TikTok gates. When you have ALL THREE audits running in the same Slack channel, the gate names are the disambiguator. **Fix:** always include the `gate_name` field in the Slack alert message; never post just "3/6 gates failed".
